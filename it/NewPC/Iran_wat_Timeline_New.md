### April 6, 2026

**All Day** — Iran officially rejects the U.S. ceasefire proposal, demanding a permanent end to the war rather than a temporary pause. Tehran's counter-proposal includes demands for U.S. troops to leave the region, Iranian control over the Strait of Hormuz, and sanctions relief. President Trump responds by threatening further strikes on Iran's infrastructure, including power plants and bridges, if the Strait remains closed.

### April 7, 2026

**23:32** — April 7, 2026  President Trump announces a two-week ceasefire with Iran, suspending all bombing and attacks contingent on Iran's agreement to the complete, immediate, and safe opening of the Strait of Hormuz. The ceasefire follows conversations with Pakistani leadership and Iran's submission of a 10-point peace proposal. Israel's Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu supports the U.S.-Iran ceasefire but states the agreement does not include Lebanon, meaning Israeli operations against Hezbollah will continue.

### April 8, 2026

**Morning** — Iran releases its 10-point peace proposal following the ceasefire agreement. Key demands include: permanent end to the war, U.S. troops to leave the region, Iranian control over the Strait of Hormuz, right to nuclear enrichment maintained, sanctions relief, safe passage in the Strait under Iranian coordination, and compensation for war damages. Iran also proposes charging fees of up to $2 million per ship transiting the strait to fund reconstruction.

**14:01** — April 8, 2026  President Trump accuses CNN of publishing a knowingly false statement attributed to Iranian officials, claiming the false report originated from a fake news website based in Nigeria. CNN stood by its reporting despite Trump's rebuke and threat of investigation.

**18:00+** — April 8, 2026  Israel launches its heaviest strikes on Lebanon since the conflict with Hezbollah began, hitting approximately 100 sites across the country. The attacks occurred just hours after the U.S.-Iran ceasefire took effect. Lebanon's health ministry reports at least 254 killed in the strikes, described by Israel as its most powerful operations on Lebanon. The attacks raise concerns that the fragile U.S.-Iran ceasefire could collapse.

### April 9, 2026

**All Day** — The U.S.-Iran ceasefire faces increasing strain as Israel continues strikes in Lebanon. Iran and the U.S. disagree on whether the truce covers Lebanon, where Israeli attacks against Hezbollah continue. Hezbollah was expected to pause attacks under the ceasefire but reports indicate they may have resumed. Iran also announced it has planted mines in the Strait of Hormuz main channel, complicating the Strait's reopening. Iran has designated alternative routes for ships but the safety of these routes remains uncertain.
