# Drone Insurance: Own-Site Business Use (UK)

*Written By:* Steve Irwin  
*Date:* 2026-05-06  
*Regulatory Context:* UK CAA — Assimilated Regulation (EU) No 785/2004; Air Navigation Order 2016  

---

## The Question

> A business buys a sub-250g camera drone to inspect its own warehouse on its own private site. Does it need drone-specific insurance? Will existing Public Liability Insurance (PLI) cover it?

**Short answers:**
- **Insurance is legally required** — own-site business use counts as a commercial operation under UK law.
- **Standard PLI will not cover it** — virtually all business PLI policies contain an aviation exclusion that catches drones.

---

## Why Insurance Is Mandatory

UK drone insurance requirements derive from **Assimilated Regulation (EU) No 785/2004** (retained in UK law post-Brexit) and the **Air Navigation Order 2016**. These require third-party liability insurance for all commercial UAS operations.

The regulation provides only one exemption from mandatory insurance: drones under 20kg used **"for sport or recreational purposes only"**. Any business use — including inspecting your own assets — falls outside this exemption.

| Question | Answer |
|----------|--------|
| Is inspecting your own warehouse "commercial"? | **Yes** — the CAA defines commercial as any operation that advances a business purpose, regardless of whether payment changes hands |
| Does operating on your own property change this? | **No** — there is no own-site exemption in UK drone insurance law |
| Does sub-250g weight reduce insurance obligations? | **No** — weight thresholds affect registration and category rules, not insurance requirements |
| Minimum cover required | **€750,000 SDR** (approximately £650,000) third-party liability |

## Does Your Existing PLI Cover It?

Almost certainly not. Standard UK business Public Liability Insurance contains an **aviation exclusion clause** — and under UK law, drones are classified as **aircraft**. This means drone-related liability falls outside the scope of a standard PLI policy.

| Scenario | Covered by Standard PLI? |
|----------|--------------------------|
| Employee trips on equipment during inspection | Yes |
| Drone crashes into warehouse roof and causes fire | **No** — aviation exclusion applies |
| Drone injures a visitor or third party on site | **No** — aviation exclusion applies |
| Drone crashes onto a neighbouring property | **No** — aviation exclusion applies |
| Drone causes property damage during flight | **No** — aviation exclusion applies |

**Can PLI be extended to cover drones?** A small number of specialist insurers can add a drone endorsement to an existing PLI policy. This requires explicit written confirmation from your insurer and is not automatic. You must ask and get it in writing — do not assume.

**The insurer name on the policy must match your CAA Operator ID** — this is a compliance requirement under EC 785/2004.

## What Insurance You Actually Need

You need **aviation-compliant third-party liability insurance** that explicitly covers commercial UAS operations and satisfies Regulation (EU) 785/2004. This is separate from and in addition to standard business PLI.

### What a Compliant Policy Covers

| Cover Type | What It Is | Required? |
|------------|------------|-----------|
| **Third-party liability** | Covers damage or injury caused to others or their property | **Legally mandatory** |
| **Hull / own-damage insurance** | Covers damage to the drone itself | Optional (but sensible) |
| **Equipment cover** | Camera, batteries, controller | Optional |
| **Personal accident** | Injury to the pilot | Optional |

## Cost-Effective Options for Occasional Business Use

For a business operating a single sub-250g drone for occasional internal inspections, dedicated commercial drone insurance is low-cost:

| Option | What It Provides | Typical Cost |
|--------|-----------------|--------------|
| **BMFA membership (commercial tier)** | EC 785/2004 compliant liability cover up to £1M; included with membership for operators with under £20K drone turnover or occasional business use | ~£60–£80/year |
| **ARPAS-UK membership** | Industry body membership with access to compliant insurance schemes | Check current rates at <a href="https://arpas.uk/uav-operator-insurance/" target="_blank">ARPAS-UK</a> |
| **Specialist drone insurers** | Dedicated UAS policy; often monthly or per-flight options available | £100–£300/year for light commercial use |
| **PLI endorsement** | Extension added to existing PLI by your current insurer | Varies — requires explicit quote |

For a sub-250g drone used a handful of times per year on your own site, **BMFA commercial membership** is likely the most straightforward and cost-effective starting point.

## Compliance Checklist

Before the first flight, confirm all of the following:

- [ ] **Operator ID** obtained from CAA (required — sub-250g with camera triggers this from Jan 2026)
- [ ] **Flyer ID** obtained by pilot (free online theory test)
- [ ] **Insurance policy** in place that explicitly covers commercial UAS operations under EC 785/2004
- [ ] **Policy name matches CAA Operator ID** registrant
- [ ] **Minimum cover confirmed**: €750,000 SDR third-party liability
- [ ] **PLI checked** with your insurer in writing — confirm whether a drone endorsement is included or excluded
- [ ] **Insurance document available** during operations (CAA may request evidence)
