## UK Regulatory Requirements (CAA)

### Operating Under Open Category (CoC)

For recreational use, the CAA requires adherence to **Open Category** rules. Key points:

#### Weight Categories & Rules

| Drone Weight | Registration Required | Insurance | Operator ID |
|--------------|------------------|-----------|---------------|
| **Under 250g** | Not required if <250g | Recommended but not mandatory for Open Cat. under 250g | Not required (if <250g) |
| **250g - 4kg** | Required | Mandatory | Required |

For drones under 250g:
- No registration needed with CAA
- No insurance legally required (but highly recommended for liability)
- Can operate as recreational flyer without operator ID

#### BVLOS Considerations

The UK permits limited BVLOS operations in the **Open Category** if:
1. Drone weighs under 400g
2. Visual line of sight maintained from a suitable position
3. Operations are not in controlled airspace (CASA)
4. Not near other aircraft or beyond visual range

For roof inspections, flying from ~50m away over a warehouse roof is typically acceptable if:
- You can visually observe the drone at all times
- No people directly underneath during flight
- Away from controlled airspace corridors

**Important:** Some interpretations require a **Certificate of Competence (CoC)** for BVLOS operations. The CAA offers free online training modules covering drone safety, regulations, and emergency procedures. Completing these takes ~1-2 hours and provides a recognised certificate at no cost.

### Key UK Operational Limits

| Parameter | Limit for Open Category |
|-----------|-------------------------|
| **Maximum altitude** | 400 ft AGL (above ground level) |
| **VLOS requirement** | Must maintain visual contact with drone |
| **Maximum speed** | Typically 58 km/h (37 mph), lower for some models |
| **No-fly zones** | Airports (CASA), restricted airspaces, military areas |
| **Altitude limit** | 400 ft AGL, unless beyond visual line of sight with CAA approval |
| **People on ground** | Must not endanger people directly below the drone |

### Checking Controlled Airspace (CASA)

Before any flight, check your intended operation area against controlled airspace:

1. Visit [<a href="https://uk-live.gov.uk/" target="_blank">UK Live</a>] (UK Live App)
2. Or use free apps like **Skyward** or **AirMap UK**
3. Check for nearby airports, helipads, MOD sites

**Yorkshire Airspace Considerations:**
- Leeds Bradford Airport (umbrella zone radius: 4 NM / ~7.5 km)
- York Airport (umbrella zone radius: 4 NM / ~7.5 km)
- HLF airfield (Humberside), RAF bases nearby
- Check your specific site location before each flight

## Legal Considerations & Compliance

### Registration Requirements (CAA UK)

For drones under 250g operated by recreational flyers:
- **No registration required** with CAA
- **No Operator ID needed** if not conducting commercial activities

**Important:** If you ever transition to commercial work or operate drones between 250g-4kg, then:
1. Register with CAA: [<a href="https://www.gov.uk/register-drone" target="_blank">Register Drone with CAA</a>]
2. Obtain insurance certificate
3. Apply for Operator ID and display on drone

### Insurance Considerations

While not legally required for drones under 250g operating in Open Category:

- **Third-party liability insurance** is strongly recommended
- Cost: ~£100-£400/year depending on coverage limits
- Site may require proof of insurance even if not legally mandated
- Consider business interruption or property damage coverage

### Privacy/GDPR Compliance

When flying over sites with employees/visitors:

1. **No filming people** in identifiable detail when not relevant to inspection
2. **Disable camera recording** during flights that don't require documentation
3. **Limit data retention** - delete footage not needed for reports promptly
4. **Display GDPR notice** at site if you're collecting personal data via drone
5. **Obtain consent** if you need to record individuals (for inspection context)

### Operational Best Practices

| Practice | Why It Matters |
|----------|----------------|
| Check UK Live airspace app before each flight | Prevents entering controlled airspace without permission |
| Fly only during daylight or twilight (dusk/dawn) | Regulatory requirement for visual observation |
| Maintain visual line of sight with drone at all times | BVLOS restrictions in Open Category |
| Never fly higher than 400 ft AGL unless authorised | CAA altitude limit for recreational flying |
| Keep flight logs of operations performed | Useful for demonstrating compliance and insurance claims |
| Report accidents/incidents to CAA if required | Even non-injury incidents should be documented |

### No-Fly Zones (Common UK Sites)

Avoid flying near or over:
- **Airports** (check CASA maps on [<a href="https://uk-live.gov.uk/" target="_blank">UK Live</a>])
- **Military airfields** (RAF bases, MOD sites)
- **Heliports** (commercial and public helipads)
- **Government buildings** in restricted zones
- **Crowded areas** or large gatherings

## Getting Started: Step-by-Step

### Week 1: Acquisition & Setup

1. **Purchase the drone:** Choose Mini 4 Pro (~£900-£1,200) from official DJI UK store or authorised UK retailer
2. **Download DJI Fly App:** Available on iOS/Android; pre-installed on most controllers
3. **Initial setup:** Follow app instructions to calibrate drone's IMU (compass calibration - may require moving away from magnetic interference)
4. **Configure firmware:** Ensure latest firmware installed via DJI Assistant 2 software or within the app

### Week 2: Training & Familiarisation

1. **Free CAA training modules:** Visit [<a href="https://www.gov.uk/find-drone-advice/training-qualification-and-certificates" target="_blank">CAA Training</a>]
   - No cost for basic modules (approx 1-2 hours total)
   - Provides Certificate of Competence (CoC) at no charge
2. **Practice flying:** Start in open, flat areas away from airspace restrictions
3. **Review local regulations:** Check your site against UK Live airspace maps

### Week 3: Test Operations

1. **Begin inspections** following the checklist below
2. **Build familiarity** with the drone's capabilities and limitations
3. **Document procedures** for consistency in future inspections

## Inspection Flight Procedures

### Pre-flight Checklist

- [ ] Check UK Live airspace status for your location
- [ ] Ensure batteries are fully charged
- [ ] Verify firmware is current on drone controller app
- [ ] Confirm wind conditions are light (under 25 mph recommended)
- [ ] Identify safe takeoff/landing zone free of people and obstacles

### During Inspection Flight

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Calibrate compass if prompted (follow on-screen directions) |
| 2 | Establish GPS lock - wait for Ready status |
| 3 | Enable obstacle avoidance sensors |
| 4 | Set flight mode to **Normal** (A2/Auto) or **Sport** only if qualified and legal |
| 5 | For roof inspection: Fly toward warehouse, maintain visual contact, fly within ~40-120ft altitude for best photos |

### Recommended Flight Pattern

```
Takeoff → Approach warehouse from non-restricted direction → Fly at 80-120ft altitude → Capture images of all roof bays → Maintain visual line of sight throughout flight → Return to home position after inspection complete
```

**Altitude:** 80-120 ft (25-35 m) typically provides optimal coverage and image quality for warehouse roofs.

## Post-Flight Procedures

### Image Review & Reporting

1. **Transfer photos/videos** from drone SD card to computer/external storage
2. **Organise by date and location** - create inspection report folder structure:
   ```
   \Warehouse Inspections\
      \YYYY-MM-DD\
         \Warehouse_A\
            \| Roof_Detail_Images.zip
            \| Inspection_Report.pdf
   ```

3. **Create inspection reports:**
   - Date, time, weather conditions
   - Drone model and firmware version
   - Photos showing roof condition
   - Notes on damage/defects observed
   - Recommendations (if applicable)

### Data Management & GDPR Compliance

- **Delete unnecessary footage** promptly from drone SD card
- **Secure photos** containing identifiable individuals if required by policy
- **Maintain audit trail** of operations and data management procedures
