## Headline: The Strait of Hormuz Crisis

On **March 4, 2026**, Iran closed the Strait of Hormuz following the outbreak of military conflict on February 28, 2026. This is **the largest single disruption to world energy supply since the 1970s energy crisis**. The Strait carries approximately 20% of global oil supplies daily — roughly 21 million barrels per day.

**Key production losses:**
- Kuwait, Iraq, Saudi Arabia, and UAE collectively lost **6.7 million barrels/day by March 10**, rising to at least **10 million barrels/day by March 12**
- 80% of Hormuz-region oil flows to Asia (Japan, South Korea, India, China)
- LNG exports also stranded, compounding the energy shock

**As of April 9-10, 2026:** The Strait remains partially closed. Iran controls access following a ceasefire, but normal tanker transit has not resumed.

## Western Canadian Select (WCS) Differential

WCS — Canada's heavy oil benchmark — trades at a discount to WTI due to:
- Higher sulphur and viscosity requiring additional refining
- Transportation costs (pipeline to US Gulf Coast)
- Competition for heavy crude refining capacity

**April 2026 differential:**
- WCS settled at **$15.55/bbl below WTI** (April 10)
- Widened to $16.15/bbl on April 7 — steepest discount since early 2024
- Narrowed slightly to $14.90 on April 8 after temporary ceasefire news

**Why the differential is widening:** US Strategic Petroleum Reserve releases (to offset Iran supply losses) are flooding US refineries with light sweet crude, reducing their appetite for Canadian heavy oil.

*Note: Pre-crisis WCS differential was typically $10–20/bbl. See `Canada_Oil_Briefing.md` for full WCS background.*

## OPEC+ Position

**Pre-crisis stance:** OPEC+ had agreed at November 2025 meeting to hold group-wide 2026 output steady with a capacity mechanism.

**March 2026:** With the Iran conflict erupting, eight OPEC+ members (Saudi Arabia, Russia, Iraq, UAE, Kuwait, Kazakhstan, Algeria, Oman) agreed to a **+206,000 bpd production adjustment** from April.

**April 2026:** OPEC+ agreed to **another +206,000 bpd quota increase** from May — widely described as a "symbolic" gesture given actual Gulf production losses of millions of barrels/day.

**OPEC+ warning:** Damage to Middle East energy assets will have a **prolonged impact** even after the war ends — "restoring damaged energy assets to full capacity is both costly and takes a long time."

## Canadian Insulation and Exposure

**Canada's relative insulation (strengths):**
- 4th largest oil producer globally (5.2 Mb/d), 3rd–4th in proven reserves
- No exposure to Hormuz route for Canadian production
- Trans Mountain Expansion (TMX) enables ~400 kb/d to Asia — now highly valued as alternative supply
- US tariff on Canadian energy removed (see Canada_Oil_Briefing.md)

**Canada's exposure (vulnerabilities):**
- Canadian refineries still need crude inputs — elevated WTI prices feed through to refining costs
- CAD/USD exchange rate amplifies crude price volatility for Canadian buyers
- Diesel import dependency in some Atlantic provinces
