## The Central Fuel for Canadian Logistics

Diesel is the lifeblood of the Canadian transportation sector. Nearly all heavy trucking, rail, marine, and much of air cargo infrastructure runs on diesel or its close derivative (jet fuel). Understanding where diesel prices are and where they're going is the core intelligence this audience needs.

## Diesel Crack Spreads — Refinery Margins

The **crack spread** is the profit margin a refinery earns turning crude oil into diesel. It signals supply tightness in the product market independent of crude oil prices.

| Period | ULSD Crack Spread | Assessment |
|---|---|---|
| Historical norm (2019–2023) | $20–$30/bbl | Normal operating environment |
| Early 2026 (pre-crisis) | ~$25–$30/bbl | Softening from 2022 peaks |
| **April 2026 (current)** | **$44–$50/bbl** | Severely elevated; structural deficit |

**What this means:** Even if crude oil prices eased, diesel would remain expensive because **global diesel supply is structurally tight** — driven by:
- Asia diesel shortages (Gulf supply stranded)
- European refineries at capacity serving both local and Asian demand
- Physical diesel being shipped from UK and US West Coast to Asia — price spread between Asian and European diesel exploded to **$244/tonne** (near zero pre-crisis)

**Refinery beneficiaries:** Valero, Phillips 66, Marathon Petroleum posted **25–28% YTD stock gains** in early 2026 as crack spreads doubled or tripled.

Sources: <a href="https://www.kpler.com/blog/softer-gasoil-diesel-values-drag-refining-margins-lower-in-the-us-and-asia" target="_blank">Kpler: Refinery Margins Feb 2026</a> | <a href="https://petrolodex.com/insights/2026-oil-gas-outlook-what-the-diesel-gasoline-and-jet-fuel-markets-are-actually-doing" target="_blank">Petrolodex: 2026 Oil & Gas Market Outlook</a>

## The CAD/USD Currency Effect

Canadian carriers and shippers face a **double exposure** to the fuel price crisis:

1. **Crude prices are USD-denominated** — Canadian refineries buy crude at USD prices, then pass through to Canadian pump prices
2. **The Canadian dollar has weakened** — USD/CAD is 1.3864 (April 8, 2026); CAD has lost ~2% in the past month

**Practical impact:** Every 1-cent weakening in the CAD against USD adds approximately **1–1.5 cents/litre** to Canadian diesel rack prices at the wholesale level.

The current CAD weakness amplifies the already-elevated crude price impact on Canadian operators.

Sources: <a href="https://www.bankofcanada.ca/rates/exchange/daily-exchange-rates/" target="_blank">Bank of Canada: Daily Exchange Rates</a> | <a href="https://www.nbc.ca/content/dam/bnc/taux-analyses/analyse-eco/mensuel/forex.pdf" target="_blank">National Bank: Forex Economics April 2026</a>

## Seasonal Demand Patterns

| Season | Canadian Diesel Demand | Typical Price Effect |
|---|---|---|
| Winter (Nov–Feb) | Peak heating oil demand; heavy road freight | +5–10 cents/L premium |
| Spring (Mar–May) | Construction/agricultural season begins | Sustained high demand |
| Summer (Jun–Aug) | High trucking, reduced heating demand | Moderate |
| Fall (Sep–Oct) | Harvest logistics; pre-winter inventory build | Building |

**Current timing (April 2026):** Canada is entering the spring demand surge (construction, agriculture) at the same time as the worst supply disruption in 50 years. This is a **worst-case seasonal timing** for price pressure.

## Canadian Diesel Supply Chain

- Canadian refineries process approximately **380,000–400,000 bpd** of crude into products
- Canada has **17 refineries** operating at ~90% utilisation (see Canada_Oil_Briefing.md for detail)
- Atlantic Canada (NL, NS, NB) has partial import dependency — exposed to global spot markets
- Alberta and Ontario refineries are better insulated by domestic crude access

**Supply disruption risk:** While Canada doesn't route crude through Hormuz, it does rely on global product markets for price signals and, in some regions, for product imports.
