## SEGMENT 1: Setting the Scene — Where We Are Now (6 min)

### 1A. The Hormuz Shock in Numbers

*The single biggest story driving everything.*

- On **March 4, 2026**, the Strait of Hormuz effectively closed
- **20% of the world's daily oil supply** (21 million barrels/day) disrupted
- Gulf producers (Saudi Arabia, Kuwait, UAE, Iraq) lost **6.7–10 million barrels/day of output**
- This is the **largest energy supply disruption since the 1970s oil crisis**
- Brent crude went from ~$71/bbl in February to a crisis peak of **$119.50/bbl on March 9** — in under two weeks

*Sources: <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2026_Strait_of_Hormuz_crisis" target="_blank">Wikipedia: 2026 Strait of Hormuz Crisis</a> | <a href="https://www.dallasfed.org/research/economics/2026/0320" target="_blank">Dallas Fed: Hormuz Closure Economic Impact</a> | <a href="https://www.eia.gov/pressroom/releases/press586.php" target="_blank">EIA Press Release April 7, 2026</a> | Brent price history: <a href="https://fred.stlouisfed.org/series/DCOILBRENTEU" target="_blank">FRED: Brent Crude Daily Prices</a>*

### 1B. Crude Oil Right Now

- **WTI:** ~$97.87/bbl (April 9–10)
- **Brent:** ~$95.92/bbl — pulled back from the $119.50 crisis peak (March 9); still up ~35% from pre-crisis
- **Western Canadian Select (WCS):** ~$82/bbl — trading at $15.55 discount to WTI
- **Key nuance:** Canadian crude is actually not routing through Hormuz. But crude is a global market. Everyone pays the global price.

### 1C. What's Happening to Your Diesel Pump Price

- Canada national average: **CAD $2.35/L** (April 6)
- BC: **up to $2.48/L**
- That is a **~43% year-on-year increase** in your single biggest variable cost
- And it's not just crude — **diesel crack spreads** (refinery margins) are at **$44–50/bbl**, more than double the historical norm
  - Translation: Even if crude eases, diesel stays expensive because global supply is structurally short

### 1D. Jet Fuel — Same Story, Higher Altitude

- Global average jet fuel: **~$209/bbl** (up ~35% from pre-crisis; rose 7.1% week-on-week in latest IATA reading)
- US spot Jet A: **$4.23–$4.88/gallon**
- For shippers using air freight (pharma, electronics, perishables): air cargo surcharges up **25–40%** above pre-crisis levels
- Both diesel and jet fuel are **middle distillates competing for the same refinery output** — refineries cannot make more of both simultaneously

*Sources: <a href="https://www.iata.org/en/publications/economics/fuel-monitor/" target="_blank">IATA: Jet Fuel Price Monitor</a> | <a href="https://www.oilpriceapi.com/live/jet-fuel-price" target="_blank">OilPriceAPI: Jet Fuel Price Live</a> | <a href="https://www.airlines.org/dataset/argus-us-jet-fuel-index/" target="_blank">Airlines for America: Argus US Jet Fuel Index</a> | <a href="https://markets.financialcontent.com/dptribune/article/marketminute-2026-4-3-sky-high-costs-airline-industry-stunned-as-jet-fuel-surges-amidst-408-national-gas-average" target="_blank">MarketMinute: Airline Industry Fuel Surge April 2026</a>*

## SEGMENT 2: What's Driving It (7 min)

### 2A. The Supply Shock — Three Layers

**Layer 1: The Hormuz closure**
- 21 Mb/d of flow disrupted
- Asia took the hardest hit: Japan (**88%** of crude via Hormuz), South Korea (**70%**), India (**65%**), China (**~30%**)
- These countries are scrambling for alternatives from West Africa and the Americas — bidding up everything

*Sources: <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2026_Strait_of_Hormuz_crisis" target="_blank">Wikipedia: 2026 Strait of Hormuz Crisis</a> | <a href="https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/11/strait-of-hormuz-closure-shipping-economy-oil.html" target="_blank">CNBC: Hormuz Closure Economic Impact March 11, 2026</a>*

**Layer 2: OPEC+ — symbolism vs. reality**
- OPEC+ agreed to raise quotas by **+206,000 bpd** in April and again in May
- That's a rounding error against 6.7–10 million bpd of lost Gulf production
- OPEC+'s own warning: *"restoring damaged energy assets to full capacity is both costly and takes a long time"*

*Sources: <a href="https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-04-05/opec-plans-symbolic-output-quota-hike-amid-war-delegates-say" target="_blank">Bloomberg: OPEC+ Plans Symbolic Quota Hike April 5, 2026</a> | <a href="https://themiddleeastinsider.com/2026/03/13/opec-production-decision-march-2026/" target="_blank">Middle East Insider: OPEC+ Production Decision March 2026</a>*

**Layer 3: The structural diesel deficit**
- Asian diesel shortages so acute that traders are shipping fuel from the UK and US West Coast to Asia
- The price spread between Asian and European diesel: **$244/tonne** — was near zero before the crisis

*Source: <a href="https://petrolodex.com/insights/2026-oil-gas-outlook-what-the-diesel-gasoline-and-jet-fuel-markets-are-actually-doing" target="_blank">Petrolodex: 2026 Oil & Gas Market Outlook</a>*

*[Slide: Supply disruption visual — global flow map showing Hormuz; before/after]*

### 2B. The Demand Side — Complicated

- **Tariff war effect:** Global trade uncertainty has softened freight demand in some segments
- **Modal shift:** High air freight costs are pushing more cargo to road — adding to diesel demand
- **US SPR releases:** Temporary; the Strategic Petroleum Reserve is finite
- **Net result:** Lower demand from trade uncertainty is not offsetting the supply shock — not even close

*Sources: <a href="https://www.eia.gov/outlooks/steo/" target="_blank">EIA: April 2026 Short-Term Energy Outlook</a> | <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Economic_impact_of_the_2026_Iran_war" target="_blank">Wikipedia: Economic Impact of the 2026 Iran War</a>*

### 2C. The Canadian Currency Effect

- USD/CAD is **1.3864** — the Canadian dollar has lost **~2%** in the past month
- Crude is priced in USD. Refinery inputs cost more. Canadian pump prices amplified.
- **Every 1-cent weakening of CAD vs. USD adds ~1–1.5 cents/litre** to your diesel
- Canada's relative insulation: no Hormuz exposure, Trans Mountain Expansion giving Canadian crude Pacific market access — but Canadian operators still pay global prices

*Sources: <a href="https://www.bankofcanada.ca/rates/exchange/daily-exchange-rates/" target="_blank">Bank of Canada: Daily Exchange Rates</a> | <a href="https://tradingeconomics.com/canada/currency" target="_blank">Trading Economics: Canadian Dollar</a> | <a href="https://www.nbc.ca/content/dam/bnc/taux-analyses/analyse-eco/mensuel/forex.pdf" target="_blank">National Bank: Forex Economics April 2026</a>*

## SEGMENT 3: Where It's Going — Three Scenarios (8 min)

**Opening:** "I'm not going to give you a single price forecast and pretend we know. What I'm going to do is give you three scenarios, tell you what triggers each one, and give you the price implications — so you can make your own informed decisions."

### Scenario 1 — BULL CASE: "The Crisis Deepens" (20–25% probability)

**What would have to happen:**
- Hormuz remains effectively closed beyond June
- Gulf infrastructure stays damaged; production cannot recover
- Conflict widens; additional energy assets targeted
- SPR exhausted; OPEC+ non-Gulf spare capacity insufficient

**What you'd see in the market:**
- Brent crude: $130–$150+/bbl
- Canadian diesel pump: **CAD $3.00–$3.50/L**
- FSC rates: 40–45%+ of linehaul
- Carrier capacity exits the market; freight rates spike

**Who this hurts most:** Mid-size carriers without hedging; shippers with spot market dependency; anyone on fixed-price contracts on the buy side

*Note: Bull/base/bear probability ranges and Canadian diesel scenario prices are Portland Fuel estimates based on scenario modelling.*

### Scenario 2 — BASE CASE: "Gradual Resolution" (50–55% probability)

**What would have to happen:**
- Hormuz restrictions ease by May–June (partial tanker transit resumes)
- Gulf production recovers 50–60% by Q3, near-normal by Q4
- Diplomatic process contains conflict
- Demand softens modestly but no hard recession

**What you'd see in the market:**
- Brent crude: peaks **~$115/bbl Q2**, eases to **$70–$85/bbl by Q4 2026**
- WTI: **~$79/bbl** full-year 2026 average
- Canadian diesel pump: **CAD $2.00–$2.20/L by Q4** — down from current but still elevated
- FSC rates fall back toward 18–22% range by year end

**Who this helps:** Carriers who survive Q2 see margin recovery in H2; shippers get FSC relief in H2

*Sources: <a href="https://www.eia.gov/outlooks/steo/pdf/steo_full.pdf" target="_blank">EIA: April 2026 STEO Full PDF</a> | <a href="https://www.thestreet.com/investing/goldman-sachs-reset-oil-price-forecast-for-the-rest-of-2026" target="_blank">TheStreet: Goldman Sachs 2026 Oil Forecast Reset</a> | <a href="https://www.investing.com/news/commodities-news/goldman-lifts-oil-price-forecast-amid-hormuz-disruptions-lower-inventories-4539959" target="_blank">Investing.com: Goldman Lifts Oil Forecast — Hormuz</a>*

### Scenario 3 — BEAR CASE: "Swift Resolution + Recession" (20–25% probability)

**What would have to happen:**
- Rapid diplomatic breakthrough; Hormuz fully open by May
- OPEC+ unity breaks; overproduction resumes
- Trade war triggers a broader recession; freight volumes fall
- Oil demand revised materially downward

**What you'd see in the market:**
- Brent crude: **$55–$70/bbl** by H2 2026
- Canadian diesel pump: **CAD $1.70–$1.90/L**
- FSC rates near pre-crisis levels (~15–18%)

**The catch:** Cheap fuel + recession = volume collapse. A carrier's revenue problem doesn't get fixed by lower fuel cost if freight volumes shrink 15–20%.

### What to Watch

Give your team these indicators to track which scenario is playing out:

1. **Strait of Hormuz shipping traffic** — <a href="https://www.marinetraffic.com" target="_blank">MarineTraffic</a> AIS data; tanker transits are real-time

## SEGMENT 4: What To Do About It (7 min)

*Transition:* "Now let's get practical. Part 3 is going to go deeper on operational strategy — Robin and Thomas will cover a lot of the tactical ground. I want to focus here on the fuel-specific tools."

### 4A. The Financial Instruments (for larger organisations)

| Tool | Best For | Key Point |
|---|---|---|
| Futures (NYMEX ULSD) | 50+ truck fleets | Requires margin management; daily cash flow |
| Swaps | Mid-large fleets | Settles against monthly average; no margin calls |
| Call options/collars | Any size, but premium cost | Cap your exposure; keep upside if prices fall |

*Sources: <a href="https://www.cmegroup.com/education/articles-and-reports/introduction-to-crack-spreads.html" target="_blank">CME Group: Introduction to Crack Spreads</a> | <a href="https://www.mercatusenergy.com/blog/bid/81549/a-beginners-guide-to-fuel-hedging-futures" target="_blank">Mercatus Energy: Beginner's Guide to Fuel Hedging</a> | <a href="https://www.breakthroughfuel.com/blog/fuel-hedging-strategies-for-market-stability/" target="_blank">Breakthrough: Fuel Hedging and Risk Management</a>*

**Honest advice:** In April 2026, with prices near a peak and massive uncertainty, **buying long-term fixed-price exposure right now is expensive**. Option premiums are elevated. This is not the moment to lock in $2.35/L for 12 months. Wait for the Q2 peak to pass if possible.

### 4B. Commercial Tools (for any fleet size)

- **Fuel cards:** 2–5 cents/L discount; immediate; any size fleet
- **Fixed-price supply contracts:** Hard to source right now — suppliers are reluctant in this volatility; good to pursue for Q3/Q4
- **Bulk purchasing on dips:** Tactical; needs tank capacity

### 4C. Your FSC Program — The Most Powerful Tool You Have

*This is the message carriers need to hear:*

Your fuel surcharge is your **primary hedge against fuel price volatility on the revenue side**. But only if it's working properly:

- **Is your FSC indexed to a published reference price?** (FCAC or NRCan weekly diesel price)
- **Is it adjusting weekly or bi-weekly?** Monthly is too slow in this market — you're absorbing spikes for 4–6 weeks
- **Is it transparent to your shippers?** Opaque FSC invites negotiation and distrust
- **Is it communicating proactively?** Don't let shippers be surprised by invoice changes

Current Canadian LTL FSC rates running **26–30%** of linehaul; some express carriers at **32–33%** for volume discount customers.

*Sources: <a href="https://fcafuel.org/" target="_blank">Freight Carriers Association of Canada: FSC Reference Index</a> | <a href="https://natural-resources.canada.ca/domestic-international-markets/diesel-prices" target="_blank">NRCan: Weekly Diesel Price (FSC reference)</a> | <a href="https://rollsright.ca/why-your-fuel-surcharge-is-higher-right-now-and-what-it-means-for-your-freight-costs-in-canada-2026/" target="_blank">Rolls Right: FSC Rates Canada 2026</a> | <a href="https://www.loomisexpress.com/loomship/Services/FuelSurcharges" target="_blank">Loomis Express: Current FSC Rates</a>*

*For shippers:* Ask your carriers for their FSC methodology. It's a reasonable question. Carriers operating transparently are the ones you want to partner with long-term.

### 4D. Operational Efficiency — Durable and Immediate

The highest-ROI moves in a $2.35/L environment:

| Action | Fuel Saving | Payback |
|---|---|---|
| Speed discipline (max 100 km/h) | 8–15% | Immediate |
| Eco-driving training | 5–12% | Immediate |
| Aerodynamic kits (side skirts, fairings) | 5–10% | 12–18 months |
| Route optimisation | 3–8% | 3–6 months |
| Load optimisation | 5–10% | Immediate |

*Sources: <a href="https://truckinghq.com/fuel-price-impact-on-hauls-what-truckers-and-fleets-need-to-know-in-2026/" target="_blank">TruckingHQ: Fuel Price Impact on Hauls 2026</a> | <a href="https://fleetrabbit.com/article/diesel-fuel-price-forecast-2026-fleet-savings" target="_blank">Fleet Rabbit: Diesel Forecast 2026 and Fleet Savings</a>*

**The numbers:** A fleet achieving 20% fuel reduction saves approximately CAD $27,000/truck/year at $2.35/L (based on a Class 8 tractor consuming ~160,000–180,000 L/year). For 100 trucks, that's **$2.7 million/year saved — and it survives any price scenario**.

*Note: Fuel consumption estimate based on Class 8 long-haul average of 35–38 L/100 km at 160,000–200,000 km/year. Calculation: Portland Fuel.*

## CLOSE / KEY TAKEAWAYS (2 min)

1. **The crisis is real and unprecedented** — Hormuz closure is the biggest supply shock in 50 years; $2.35/L diesel is not a blip

2. **Three scenarios matter** — base case says H2 brings some relief; bull case (20–25%) says plan for $3/L; bear case means recession, not fuel relief

3. **Q2 is the pain point** — whether you're a carrier or shipper, the next 6–8 weeks are the hardest; manage cash accordingly

4. **Your FSC program is your hedge** — carriers: make it transparent, make it fast-adjusting; shippers: understand it, don't fight it blindly

5. **Efficiency is the universal win** — it works in every price scenario, it compounds over time, and in this market, every litre saved pays back faster than ever
