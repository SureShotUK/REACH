### CITT have posted this description on their website:

The global fuel market is in an unprecedented state of turmoil and unpredictability. Supply chain logistics professionals are faced with an array of complex problems, effecting their profitability, operations, partner relationships and beyond. 

In this special, extra-length seminar, attendees will get detailed insights into the current fuel market environment, predictions, analysis, and tangible, deployment-ready advice for staying competitive and managing margins. 

In Part 1, Heather Exner-Pirot, Director of Energy, Natural Resources and Environment at the Macdonald-Laurier Institute will discuss the geopolitics as it pertains to the current fuel/energy situation, including possible outcomes and contingencies, and what Canada and other nations could and are doing in response to the volatility. 

In Part 2, Portland Fuel will zoom in to look more specifically at the fuel markets in Canada and globally.  You'll get specific and detailed information and insights with respect to the fuel markets and how the various stakeholders are behaving. Various forecasts will be modelled and you'll also learn about fuel hedging and other strategies for mitigating risk. 

In Part 3, Thomas Allan, Director, Yield Management at Challenger Motor Freight and Robin Zhu, Director, Corporate Service at Bison Transport will give both carriers and shippers practical strategies, advice, and actionable ideas for navigating the present turmoil - and how to be better prepared for similar disruptions in the future. You'll hear how to maintain margins and remain competitive using smart strategy, technology, and more.

### CITT Brief Sent by Email

About CITT

CITT is Canada’s Logistics Association – the professional home of individuals, businesses and academic institutions involved in logistics, transportation and distribution. CITT provides training and certification, corporate solutions, as well as learning & networking opportunities for those in the industry, including its acclaimed annual Canada’s Logistics Conference. 
Created by industry, for industry in 1958, CITT has been a proud champion of the industry and bolstering the effectiveness and competitiveness of Canada’s logistics operations and supply chains for nearly seven decades.
About the Audience

CITT’s online seminars are attended by both our members and our audience of non-members interested in keeping abreast of industry issues. Attendees skew senior and typically are at the Sr. Manager level through the C-suite. About 60% of the attendees of our typical learning events are transportation service providers (“carriers”), from private trucking fleets, railways, marine and air transportation.

The remainder are from businesses for which logistics supports their primary business – including retail, mining, agriculture, CPG, pharma, petrochemicals, food & beverage and more. While some of these businesses have private trucking fleets, many of them outsource most or all of their transportation needs to carrier companies. 

A small portion of attendees are consultants, academics, and service providers to the logistics industry such as in technology or law.

About the 2026 Fuel Brief

The 2026 Fuel Brief is intended to give Canadian logistics decision-makers information, context, and best practices to help their operations navigate this unpredictable moment in the global economy. While nearly every business has been or will be affected in some way, logistics/transportation is obviously particularly sensitive.

The Brief is a three-part seminar. Your part will be the second. Part 1 is a look at the geopolitical sparks and the nation-level response. Part 3 is a detailed look at tactics and strategies transportation companies can use to mitigate risk and optimize margins, presented by yield management experts at major Canadian carriers. 

For Part 2, we’ll want to give attendees a detailed look at the fuel markets as they stand and where they might be going – particularly with any specific relevance to the transportation sector. Facts and data are essential, but attendees will also appreciate potential scenario modeling, informed forecasts, contingencies, and insights on leading organization/best practices for navigating the current market. While we know the future is very uncertain, you wouldn’t go amiss with expert speculation on market behaviours.
