# UK National Emergency Plan for Fuel (NEP-F)
*Department for Energy Security & Net Zero (DESNZ) | <a href="https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/energy-emergency-plans-priority-fuel-allocation/summary-of-response-tools-in-the-national-emergency-plan-for-fuel" target="_blank">Source: GOV.UK</a>*

---

## What it is

- The **NEP-F** sets out government options, priorities and principles for responding to a major fuel supply disruption in Great Britain
- Covers both **statutory and non-statutory** measures that government can deploy to support industry
- Principal aim: **maintain fuel supplies as close to normal levels as possible**
- Produced by DESNZ; activation can only be decided at **national level**, led by DESNZ

---

## Objectives

- Protect human life and, as far as possible, property
- Alleviate suffering
- Support continuity of everyday activity and restoration of disrupted services
- Uphold the rule of law and democratic process

---

## Activation threshold

- Reserved for **significant and widespread disruption** to oil supply only
- Preference for the **least invasive measure first**
- Emergency powers under the **Energy Act 1976** available but reserved for the most severe disruptions
- Local Resilience Fora must have their own business continuity plans and **should not rely on early NEP-F activation**

---

## Tools 1–5: Measures to maintain fuel supply

- **1. Downstream Oil Industry Protocol** — temporarily exempts industry from the Competition Act 1998; enables information sharing, joint planning, and co-ordinated supply action
- **2. Reserve Tanker Fleet** — government reserve fleet of fuel tankers deployable at short notice to supplement industry capacity
- **3. Reserve Tanker Drivers** — Armed Forces capability maintained to make fuel deliveries in the event of serious disruption to normal deliveries
- **4. Relaxation of Drivers' Hours** — must be requested by industry; can only be approved by the Department for Transport (DfT); limited to specific operations and time periods
- **5. Oil Stocks Release** — UK emergency stocks released to provide additional supply; only used for **significant disruption to global supply** to calm the market

---

## Tools 6–10: Measures to control supply and demand
*(activated only in a severe national fuel supply shortage)*

- **6. Designated Filling Station Scheme** — emergency and critical service vehicles given priority access at filling stations designated by Local Resilience Fora
- **7. Bulk Distribution Scheme** — oil companies directed to prioritise bulk petroleum delivery to **critical services** (emergency services, utilities, public transport)
- **8. Commercial Distribution Scheme** — oil companies directed to prioritise **road diesel to commercial vehicles** supporting key supply chains (food, health)
- **9. Maximum Purchase Scheme** — restricts retail road fuel sales to a **maximum amount per visit**; can also limit hours of sale to ensure all motorists retain access to some fuel
- **10. Crude Oil and Imported Product Allocation Scheme** — government formally allocates crude oil and imported oil products within the UK

---

*Legal basis for control measures (Tools 6–10): Energy Act 1976*
