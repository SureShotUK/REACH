### Hazard Classification (UK CLP)
- **H226** - Flammable liquid and vapour (Category 3)
- **H304** - May be fatal if swallowed and enters airways (Aspiration Toxicity Category 1)

## COSHH (Control of Substances Hazardous to Health)

### Important Note
**No specific HSE COSHH Essentials sheet exists for HVO.** Employers must conduct site-specific COSHH risk assessments using information from Safety Data Sheets.

### COSHH Assessment Requirements

A COSHH assessment for HVO must consider:

**Hazard Information (from SDS):**
- Aspiration hazard (H304) - fatal if swallowed and enters airways
- Combustible liquid (flash point >60°C)
- Skin irritation from repeated exposure

**Site-Specific Factors:**
- Quantities stored and handled
- Storage conditions (bulk tanks, containers)
- Handling methods (dispensing, refueling, maintenance)
- Ventilation (enclosed spaces vs. open air)
- Potential for exposure (spillage, vapor, skin contact)
- Emergency procedures
- Training requirements

**Risk Controls:**
- Prevent ingestion (labeled containers, no siphoning)
- Avoid creating mist/spray (pressure systems)
- Provide adequate ventilation
- Use appropriate PPE (gloves, eye protection)
- Emergency response procedures (spillage, fire)

### COSHH Resources

**HSE COSHH Guidance:**
- <a href="https://www.hse.gov.uk/coshh/" target="_blank">HSE COSHH Overview</a>
- <a href="https://www.hse.gov.uk/coshh/essentials/" target="_blank">HSE COSHH Essentials</a>

**HVO-Specific Guidance:**
- <a href="https://www.pendle.gov.uk/download/meetings/id/26369/item_15_appendix_3" target="_blank">Pendle Council: HVO Fuel Briefing Note</a> (includes COSHH considerations)
- <a href="https://sigma-hse.com/news-insights/diesel-process-safety/" target="_blank">Sigma-HSE: Diesel Process Safety</a> (includes HVO combustible liquid properties)

### Key COSHH Findings for HVO

**Flash Point:** >60°C (typically 61-74°C)
- **Classification:** Combustible liquid (not flammable under normal conditions)
- **HSG176 Separation:** Not subject to flammable liquid storage separation requirements
- **However:** Pressure spray/mist can create flammable conditions (high surface area droplets)

**Storage:**
- Standard petroleum storage requirements apply
- No special low-flashpoint precautions required
- Adequate ventilation for vapor control
- Bunding for spill containment

**Use in Vehicles:**
- Drop-in replacement for conventional diesel
- Same refueling precautions as diesel
- Lower emissions profile
- Training may be needed on HVO-specific properties

## Physical Properties (Typical Values)

**Appearance:** Clear, colorless to pale yellow liquid
**Odor:** Mild, hydrocarbon odor
**Flash Point:** 61-74°C (typically >60°C)
**Auto-ignition Temperature:** 200-280°C
**Density:** 775-785 kg/m³ at 15°C
**Cetane Number:** >70 (higher than conventional diesel)
**Sulfur Content:** <10 ppm (ultra-low sulfur)
**FAME Content:** 0% (not biodiesel)

**Note:** Values vary by supplier and production feedstock. Refer to specific supplier SDS for exact values.
