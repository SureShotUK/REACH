## 4. COSHH Assessment Resources

### General COSHH Information for HVO

**Key Finding:** No specific UK HSE COSHH Essentials sheet for HVO was identified. COSHH assessments must be prepared by individual businesses based on Safety Data Sheets and workplace-specific risk factors.

### Important COSHH Considerations for HVO

#### Classification Under COSHH
Based on SDS review, HVO is classified as:
- **H304:** May be fatal if swallowed and enters airways (Aspiration Hazard)
- **Combustible liquid** (flash point >60°C)
- Repeated exposure may cause skin dryness or cracking

**Process Safety Note:**
- HVO flash point is above 60°C (combustible, not flammable)
- NOT subject to separation distances of HSG176 (storage of flammable liquids)
- Less likely to produce flammable mist than conventional diesel
- However, high flash point liquids released as spray at pressure CAN produce flammable mists due to high surface area of droplets

**Source:** <a href="https://sigma-hse.com/news-insights/diesel-process-safety/" target="_blank">Sigma-HSE: Diesel Process Safety Considerations</a>

#### Health Hazards
- Oil mist may irritate eyes and respiratory tract
- Prolonged/repeated skin contact may cause dermatitis
- Aspiration hazard if swallowed
- May cause skin dryness or cracking with repeated exposure

#### Safe Handling Requirements
- Handle in closed systems or provide sufficient ventilation
- Avoid skin contact and inhalation of oil mist
- Use hydrocarbon-resistant gloves (nitrile NBR or PVC, protection index >5)
- Respiratory protection for oil mist: A2/P2 combined particle and organic vapour filter

**Source:** Multiple SDS documents referenced above

### COSHH Resources

#### General Guidance
- <a href="https://www.hse.gov.uk/coshh/" target="_blank">HSE COSHH Overview</a>
- Safety Data Sheets are the primary source for COSHH assessment data
- Employers must conduct workplace-specific risk assessments

#### HVO-Specific Briefing
- <a href="https://www.pendle.gov.uk/download/meetings/id/26369/item_15_appendix_3" target="_blank">Pendle Council: HVO Fuel Briefing</a> (Contains practical safety considerations)

### COSHH Assessment Responsibility

**Critical Note:** Safety Data Sheets contain essential information but do NOT constitute a COSHH risk assessment. Organisations must:
1. Obtain current SDS for the specific HVO product being used
2. Conduct workplace-specific risk assessment
3. Consider:
   - Quantities used/stored
   - Handling methods (bulk storage, dispensing, vehicle refueling)
   - Ventilation provisions
   - Potential for exposure (skin contact, inhalation, ingestion)
   - Emergency procedures
   - Training requirements

## 6. Key Safety Information Summary

### Hazard Classification
Based on multiple SDS sources, HVO is typically classified as:

- **H227** - Combustible liquid (Category 4 flammable liquid)
- **H304** - May be fatal if swallowed and enters airways (Aspiration hazard Category 1)
- **H315** - Causes skin irritation (some formulations)
- **H351** - Suspected of causing cancer (some formulations - Category 2)
- **H373** - May cause damage to organs through prolonged/repeated exposure (Category 2)
- **EUH066** - Repeated exposure may cause skin dryness or cracking

**Note:** Exact classification varies by supplier and specific formulation. Always refer to supplier-specific SDS.

### Composition
- Middle distillate-range iso- and n-paraffinic hydrocarbons
- C15-C18 isoalkanes predominate
- Total aromatics: typically <1.0% by weight
- Zero sulfur content
- FAME-free (no biodiesel fatty acid methyl esters)

### Storage Requirements
- Store in tanks suitable for combustible liquids
- Cool, dry location
- No direct sunlight exposure
- Shelf life: Up to 10 years (significantly longer than conventional diesel)
- Closed system handling recommended (bulk storage tanks, sealed pipework, API dry-break couplings)

### Environmental Benefits
- Greenhouse gas emissions reduced by 75-90% vs. fossil diesel (lifecycle basis)
- Biodegradable
- Zero FAME content (avoids stability/operability issues of biodiesel blends)

### Specification Compliance
- **BS EN 15940** - Paraffinic diesel fuel (PRIMARY SPECIFICATION)
- Exceeds BS EN 590 Diesel specification (except density parameter)
