### Legal Position

**After 27th October 2026, you will be in breach of Article 5 of UK REACH** - the "no data, no market" principle. This prohibits the import of substances at 1000+ tonnes/year that have not been registered according to REACH requirements.

**Importing after the deadline without registration is a criminal offense** under The REACH Enforcement Regulations 2008.

### Potential Consequences

1. **Criminal Prosecution**
   - Magistrates Court: Up to £5,000 fine and/or 3 months imprisonment
   - Crown Court: Significantly higher penalties for serious cases
   - Personal liability for directors and officers

2. **Business Disruption**
   - Forced cessation of imports
   - Seizure of non-compliant goods
   - Enforcement notices from HSE
   - Inability to fulfill customer contracts

3. **Reputational Damage**
   - Public record of non-compliance
   - Loss of customer confidence
   - Supply chain disruption

4. **Financial Impact**
   - Penalties and fines
   - Lost business during non-import period
   - Emergency compliance costs (typically higher than planned compliance)
   - Legal defense costs

### OPTION B: Submit UK REACH Registration

**Description:** Become a registrant by submitting a full UK REACH registration for Urea

**Advantages:**
- ✓ Full control over compliance
- ✓ Direct import relationships maintained
- ✓ Long-term solution
- ✓ Potential competitive advantage

**Disadvantages:**
- Very expensive (£70k-£350k+)
- Long timeline (12-24 months)
- Complex technical requirements
- Requires extensive toxicological data
- Ongoing compliance obligations

**Timeline:**
1. Months 1-3: Engage consultant, assess data needs, seek consortium
2. Months 3-9: Data gathering, gap analysis, testing if needed
3. Months 9-18: Dossier preparation (IUCLID software)
4. Months 18-21: Chemical Safety Assessment (CSA) and Report (CSR)
5. Months 21-24: Submission, HSE review, and approval

**During this period, you still cannot legally import.**

**Costs Breakdown:**

| Cost Element | Low Estimate | High Estimate | Notes |
|--------------|--------------|---------------|-------|
| HSE Registration Fee | £2,222 | £2,222 | Flat fee (or £740/£399 if SME) |
| Consortium Membership | £10,000 | £30,000 | If Urea consortium exists |
| Existing Data Purchase | £20,000 | £80,000 | Depends on consortium terms |
| New Testing (if needed) | £0 | £150,000 | Annex IX requires extensive tests |
| Regulatory Consulting | £25,000 | £60,000 | Dossier prep, CSA/CSR |
| IUCLID Software/Support | £3,000 | £8,000 | Technical submission support |
| Legal Review | £5,000 | £15,000 | Contract review, compliance |
| Ongoing Costs (annual) | £5,000 | £15,000 | Updates, renewal, monitoring |
| **TOTAL UPFRONT** | **£65,222** | **£345,222** | Wide range due to data uncertainty |

**Factors Affecting Cost:**
- **Urea consortium availability:** If a UK REACH consortium exists for Urea, costs significantly lower
- **Data sharing:** Existing registrants' data can be purchased/shared
- **Testing needs:** Annex IX requires extensive testing; if data already exists, much cheaper
- **Timeline pressure:** Emergency registrations cost more

**Action Steps:**
1. Research UK REACH Urea consortium existence
2. Engage REACH consultant for data gap analysis
3. Assess data availability and sharing opportunities
4. Determine budget and timeline feasibility
5. Decide if this is viable vs. Option A

**Risk Level:** MEDIUM-HIGH - Long timeline, high cost, complex process

## DOWNSTREAM USER OBLIGATIONS

Once you switch to a GB supplier and become a downstream user, you will have the following obligations:

### Record Keeping
- Maintain records of supplier's UK REACH registration
- Keep Safety Data Sheets (SDS) for Urea
- Document uses of Urea in your operations
- Retain for at least 10 years

### Use Verification
- Ensure your uses of Urea are covered by supplier's registration
- Identified uses must be listed in supplier's Chemical Safety Report (CSR)
- If your use is outside supplier's registered uses, you may need to submit a downstream user report

### Communication Obligations
- Inform supplier of any new information on hazards
- Report any adverse effects or incidents
- Respond to supply chain information requests

### SDS Compliance
- Follow safety instructions in SDS
- Provide SDS to your customers if you supply Urea-containing products
- Implement risk management measures

**These obligations are much simpler and less costly than being a registrant.**
