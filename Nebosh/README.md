# NEBOSH National General Certificate Study Repository

Welcome to your NEBOSH NGC study repository. This workspace is designed to support your preparation for the NEBOSH National General Certificate in Occupational Health and Safety.

## About NEBOSH NGC

The NEBOSH National General Certificate (NGC) is the UK's leading health and safety qualification, providing a comprehensive understanding of health and safety principles and practices. It consists of:

- **NG1**: Management of Health and Safety (written assessment covering 5 elements)
- **NG2**: Risk Assessment (practical workplace risk assessment)

## NGC Syllabus Elements

### NG1: Management of Health and Safety

1. **Element 1**: Why We Should Manage Workplace Health and Safety
2. **Element 2**: How Health and Safety Management Systems Work
3. **Element 3**: Managing Risk - Understanding People and Processes
4. **Element 4**: Health and Safety Management Systems - Risk Assessment
5. **Element 5**: Health and Safety Management Systems - Monitoring, Review and Audit

### NG2: Risk Assessment

Practical assessment of workplace risks with professional documentation

## Study Approach

### Key Principles
- Everything connects back to HSG65 Plan-Do-Check-Act
- Use the hierarchy of controls for all risk scenarios
- Practice the five-step risk assessment process
- Understand NGC command words (identify, outline, describe, explain)
- Apply concepts to real workplace examples

### Study Tips
- Break study into manageable chunks by syllabus element
- Practice writing timed answers
- Use active recall and spaced repetition
- Create summary sheets for quick revision
- Review NEBOSH examiner reports to understand marking
- Practice, practice, practice risk assessments

## Assessment Preparation

### NG1 Exam Technique
- Understand what each command word requires
- Structure answers with clear, separate points
- Manage time across all questions
- Use workplace examples to demonstrate understanding

### NG2 Practical
- Systematic hazard identification
- Evaluate both likelihood and severity
- Describe existing controls before recommending new ones
- Apply hierarchy of controls
- Present findings professionally
