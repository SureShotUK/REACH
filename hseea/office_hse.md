# Office Health and Safety Best Practices - One-Page Guide

## Legal Framework (UK)
**Key Regulations:**
- Health and Safety at Work etc. Act 1974
- Management of Health and Safety at Work Regulations 1999
- Workplace (Health, Safety and Welfare) Regulations 1992
- Health and Safety (Display Screen Equipment) Regulations 1992
- Manual Handling Operations Regulations 1992
- Regulatory Reform (Fire Safety) Order 2005

## Essential Compliance Requirements (MUST DO)

### 1. Health and Safety Policy
- **Legal Requirement:** Written policy if 5+ employees *(Section 2(3) of the Health and Safety at Work etc. Act 1974)*
- Display where employees can read it
- Review annually and after significant changes
- **Reference:** HSG65 "Managing for health and safety"

### 2. Risk Assessments
- **Legal Requirement:** Conduct and record assessments *(Regulation 3 of the Management of Health and Safety at Work Regulations 1999)*
- Cover all work activities and hazards
- Review annually or after incidents/changes *(Regulation 3(3) & 3(6) MHSW Regulations 1999)*
- Common office hazards: trips/slips, manual handling, DSE, fire, violence/aggression
- **Reference:** INDG163 "Five steps to risk assessment"

### 3. Display Screen Equipment (DSE)
- **Legal Requirement:** Assess workstations for regular DSE users *(Regulation 2 of the Health and Safety (Display Screen Equipment) Regulations 1992)*
- Provide adjustable chairs, adequate lighting, suitable desks *(Regulation 3 & Schedule 1, DSE Regulations 1992)*
- Offer eye tests on request (employer pays) *(Regulation 5, DSE Regulations 1992)*
- Encourage breaks: 5-10 minutes per hour away from screen *(Regulation 4, DSE Regulations 1992)*
- **Reference:** HSE Guidance L26 "Work with display screen equipment"

### 4. Fire Safety
- **Legal Requirement:** Fire risk assessment *(Article 9 of the Regulatory Reform (Fire Safety) Order 2005)*
- Maintain fire detection and alarm systems *(Article 13, RR(FS)O 2005)*
- Provide adequate fire extinguishers (serviced annually) *(Article 13, RR(FS)O 2005)*
- Ensure clear evacuation routes and assembly points *(Article 14, RR(FS)O 2005)*
- Conduct fire drills (recommended: twice yearly) *(Article 15, RR(FS)O 2005)*
- Appoint fire wardens *(Article 13(3), RR(FS)O 2005 - competent persons)*
- **Reference:** HM Government Fire Safety Risk Assessment guides

### 5. First Aid
- **Legal Requirement:** Adequate first aid provision *(Regulation 3 of the Health and Safety (First-Aid) Regulations 1981)*
- Minimum: Appointed person + first aid kit for low-risk offices *(Regulation 3(2), First-Aid Regulations 1981)*
- First aider recommended if 50+ employees or higher risk
- Display first aid notices with emergency contacts *(Regulation 4, First-Aid Regulations 1981)*
- **Reference:** L74 "First aid at work" (ACOP) and INDG214 "First aid at work: Your questions answered"

### 6. Accident Reporting
- **Legal Requirement:** Maintain accident book *(Social Security (Claims and Payments) Regulations 1979, Schedule 1)*
- Report serious incidents under RIDDOR (deaths, major injuries, 7+ day absences, dangerous occurrences) *(Reporting of Injuries, Diseases and Dangerous Occurrences Regulations 2013, Regulations 4-6)*
- Investigate incidents and implement preventive measures *(Regulation 5, MHSW Regulations 1999)*
- **Reference:** HSE31 "RIDDOR explained", L73 "A guide to RIDDOR 2013"

### 7. Information and Training
- **Legal Requirement:** Provide health and safety information and training *(Regulation 10, MHSW Regulations 1999; Section 2(2)(c), HSWA 1974)*
- Induction training for new employees *(Regulation 11, MHSW Regulations 1999)*
- Specific training for DSE users, fire wardens, first aiders *(Regulation 6, DSE Regulations 1992; Article 21, RR(FS)O 2005)*
- Refresh training periodically *(Regulation 13(2)(b), MHSW Regulations 1999)*
- **Reference:** INDG345 "Health and safety training: A brief guide"

## Best Practices (SHOULD DO)

### Workstation Setup
- Monitor at eye level, arm's length away
- Keyboard and mouse close, wrists straight
- Feet flat on floor or footrest
- Document holder at same height as screen
- Reduce glare from windows/lights

### Manual Handling
- Provide trolleys/equipment for moving supplies *(Regulation 4(1)(b)(ii), Manual Handling Operations Regulations 1992)*
- Train staff on safe lifting techniques *(Regulation 4(1)(b)(iii), MHO Regulations 1992)*
- Avoid repetitive lifting where possible *(Regulation 4(1)(a), MHO Regulations 1992 - avoid hazardous manual handling)*
- **Reference:** INDG143 "Manual handling at work: A brief guide", L23 "Manual Handling Operations Regulations 1992 Guidance"

### Welfare Facilities
- Clean toilets and washing facilities *(Regulation 20 & 21, Workplace (Health, Safety and Welfare) Regulations 1992)*
- Drinking water readily available *(Regulation 22, Workplace Regulations 1992)*
- Adequate heating (minimum 16Â°C after first hour) *(Regulation 7, Workplace Regulations 1992)*
- Appropriate lighting and ventilation *(Regulations 8 & 6, Workplace Regulations 1992)*
- Rest areas and facilities for pregnant/nursing mothers *(Regulation 25, Workplace Regulations 1992; Regulation 16(2), MHSW Regulations 1999)*
- **Reference:** L24 "Workplace health, safety and welfare" (ACOP), INDG244 "Welfare at work"

### Occupational Health
- Stress management and mental health support *(Regulation 3, MHSW Regulations 1999 - risk assessment includes psychosocial risks)*
- Ergonomic assessments for individuals with issues *(Regulation 16(1), MHSW Regulations 1999)*
- Return-to-work procedures after illness *(Equality Act 2010 - reasonable adjustments)*
- Health surveillance where required (e.g., noise exposure) *(Regulation 6, MHSW Regulations 1999)*
- **Reference:** HSG218 "Managing stress at work", INDG430 "Stress and mental health at work"

### Consultation
- Consult employees on health and safety matters *(Health and Safety (Consultation with Employees) Regulations 1996; Safety Representatives and Safety Committees Regulations 1977)*
- Consider appointing safety representatives *(Regulation 4, Consultation with Employees Regulations 1996)*
- Regular safety meetings or communications *(Regulation 3, Consultation with Employees Regulations 1996)*
- **Reference:** INDG232 "Consulting employees on health and safety: A brief guide to the law"

## Common Office Hazards and Controls

*Control measures must follow the hierarchy of controls (Schedule 1, MHSW Regulations 1999) and be proportionate to the risk.*

| Hazard | Control Measures | Key Legislation |
|--------|------------------|-----------------|
| **Slips/Trips** | Keep floors clear, secure cables, clean spills immediately, adequate lighting | Regulation 12, Workplace Regulations 1992 |
| **Falls** | No standing on chairs; use stepladders with training | Regulations 6 & 13, Work at Height Regulations 2005 |
| **DSE Issues** | Proper workstation setup, breaks, eye tests, ergonomic equipment | DSE Regulations 1992 |
| **Manual Handling** | Risk assessment, training, mechanical aids, team lifting | MHO Regulations 1992 |
| **Fire** | Regular checks, clear exits, drills, trained wardens, no blocked routes | RR(FS)O 2005 |
| **Electrical** | PAT testing, visual checks, report faults immediately | Electricity at Work Regulations 1989 |
| **Violence/Aggression** | Lone working procedures, visitor management, reporting system | Regulation 3, MHSW Regulations 1999 |
| **Stress** | Workload management, support systems, training for managers | Regulation 3, MHSW Regulations 1999 |

## Documentation to Maintain
- Health and Safety Policy (review annually)
- Risk assessments (general workplace + specific activities)
- Fire risk assessment and evacuation plan
- DSE assessments
- Accident book and RIDDOR reports
- Training records
- Fire drill records
- Equipment maintenance records (fire extinguishers, PAT testing)
- First aid and appointed person details
