# Ladder Inspection Summary #

**Prepared:** 2026-05-18  
**Context:** Comparison of quotes to inspect fixed ladders on site with a view to making a descision of which contractor to appoint.

---
## HeightSafe ##

### Standard £795, Enhanced £1,400 ###

Standard report is quite good, comes with a clear pass fail section, but doesn't seem to have any weight testing sections at all. Produces quite a good overall summary certificate that details all of the items on test for the site. Clear pass or fail in this section as well.  

For the enhanced test section, they would add a load test to the inspections that they're making whereby they would hang approximately 90 to 115 kilogram weight to the centre of the rung to simulate a worker to ensure that the ladder can accommodate the appropriate weight. This would add a bit more to the cost, but still is one of the cheapest options.   

## Altus ##

### Standard £1,895, Enhanced £5,100 ###

Standard report's quite good.Looks like it has a specific weight testing section and it has a clear pass fail mark, has some descriptions and shows the regulation, the British standard that it's tested to.   

Full Structural Report.Not quite as good. It has details on the rung spacing, style width and those kind of details that you can measure, but doesn't have any kind of structural weight testing and doesn't have a clear pass or fail section.Plus, this costs an awful lot more.   

## HSQE Consultancy ##

### Enhanced £2,901 ###

I have a bit of a superiority complex from what I can gather, seem to think that their reports are far better than anyone else's and their testing is better than anyone else's but I guess there's no harm in taking pride in your work. The report itself is very detailed. It runs over four pages per ladder, but doesn't have a clear pass or fail, and the company has said that they would provide quotes for any remedial work that was needed, with descriptions of what work that was.  

## Eurosafe UK ##

### Standard £2,055 ###

EuroSafe never provided an example report, and as they weren't the cheapest or the best service, I never really followed up or chased them for it. I basically take the view that if they can't fulfill the brief at the sales stage, then they're not going to fulfill the brief once they've got the contract. I could proceed with any of the other contractors, but not really this one on that basis.
