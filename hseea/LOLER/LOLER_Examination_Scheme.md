## 1. Purpose and Scope

This LOLER Examination Scheme sets out the requirements for thorough examination, inspection, and maintenance of all lifting equipment used at Noxdown to ensure compliance with LOLER regulations.

### Key Objectives:
- Ensure safe use of lifting equipment throughout its lifecycle
- Prevent accidents through regular competent person examinations
- Comply with <a href="https://www.legislation.gov.uk/uksi/1998/2307/regulation/9" target="_blank">Regulation 9</a> requirements for thorough examination
- Protect operators and other workers from identified risks

## 3. Examination Requirements by Regulation

### Regulation 9(3) - Periodic Thorough Examination

Thorough examination is required where equipment is subject to deterioration due to use and where this deterioration could lead to a dangerous situation.

**Examination Triggers:**
1. ✓ Before first use (if no valid Declaration of Conformity within 12 months)
2. ✓ Upon installation or re-installation
3. ✓ After exceptional circumstances (e.g., extreme weather, impact damage)
4. ✓ At regular intervals based on risk assessment and equipment type

### Examination Intervals by Equipment Type:

| Equipment | Maximum Interval | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Equipment used for lifting people | More frequent | Requires closer monitoring |
| Arduous environments (construction) | Shorter intervals | Deterioration risk higher |
| General industrial use | As per risk assessment | Manufacturer instructions apply |

## 4. Competent Person Requirements

### Definition:
A competent person is someone who has the necessary knowledge, training, and experience to carry out thorough examinations and inspections safely.

### Competent Person Responsibilities:

| Responsibility | Action Required |
|---|---|
| Determine inspection nature | Assess what inspections are required based on risk |
| Carry out inspections | Perform competent physical examination of equipment |
| Detect deterioration | Identify rapid wear from arduous environments |
| Report defects | Notify employer of dangerous defects forthwith |

### Competent Person Selection:
- Must be independent or have appropriate organizational independence
- Should have knowledge of manufacturer instructions
- Must understand relevant LOLER regulations
- Training records must be maintained

---

## 5. Examination Report Requirements

As per Regulation 9, all thorough examinations must be documented in writing.

### Required Report Content:

**Section A - Equipment Information:**
- Company name and equipment location
- Equipment identification (make, model, serial number)
- Safe working load marked on equipment
- Date of previous examination

**Section B - Examination Details:**
- Name and credentials of competent person
- Examination date and time
- Scope of examination conducted
- Any defects identified

**Section C - Risk Assessment:**
- Existing or imminent risk evaluation
- Recommendations for immediate action
- Proposed remedial actions with timelines

**Section D - Certification:**
- Authentication by signature or equally secure means
- Company acknowledgment section
- Date and signature of competent person

### Report Distribution:

| Recipient | When | Format |
|---|---|---|
| Operations Manager | As soon as practicable | Written/Secure electronic |
| Third Party Owner (if hired/leased) | As soon as practicable | Written/Secure electronic |
| Enforcing Authority | If serious personal injury risk exists | Immediate report |

## 6. Defect and Hazard Notification Procedures

### Immediate Actions Required When Defects are Identified:

**Level 1 - Dangerous to Persons (Immediate Action):**
1. Notify the Operations Manager forthwith
2. Immediately take equipment out of service
3. Tag "DO NOT USE" and restrict access
4. Initiate incident investigation if required

**Level 2 - Defects Requiring Remedial Action:**
1. Document defect in examination report
2. Set deadline for remediation
3. Re-inspect after repairs completed
4. Update equipment log

**Level 3 - Enforcing Authority Notification:**
Send copy of report to relevant enforcing authority if there is an existing or imminent risk of serious personal injury.

## 7. Pre-Use and First-Time Examination Requirements

### Before First Use:
- Essential when no valid Declaration of Conformity made within last 12 months
- Complete initial inspection checklist required
- All safety devices must be tested and functional

### After Installation/Re-installation:
- Verify proper assembly at new site
- Test all lifting mechanisms
- Confirm load markings are visible and correct
- Document installation inspection completion

---

## 8. Risk Assessment Integration

Risk assessment determines the nature and frequency of inspections required [4].

### Risk Factors to Consider:

| Factor | Impact on Examination Frequency |
|---|---|
| Arduous environment (e.g., construction) | More frequent inspection required |
| Equipment used for lifting people | Shorter examination intervals |
| Deterioration potential | Periodic thorough examination mandatory |
| Manufacturer recommendations | Refer to instructions when determining scope |

### Conditions Detected by Inspection:
- (a) Rapid wear arising from use in arduous environments
- Equipment condition deterioration over time
- Failure of safety devices or components
- Wear and tear affecting safe operation

---

## 9. Maintenance and Intermediate Inspections

In addition to thorough examinations, maintenance is required to ensure equipment remains safe.

### Interim Inspection Requirements:

| When | Frequency | Who |
|---|---|---|
| Between thorough examinations | As determined by risk assessment | Competent person |
| By operator | Regular visual checks | Trained operator |
| By maintenance personnel | Scheduled preventive maintenance | Maintenance staff |

### Maintenance Requirements (PUWER):
- Maintain equipment in safe working order
- Detect deterioration early to remedy in good time
- Records of all maintenance activities must be kept

### Documentation Standards:

---

## 11. Equipment Out of Business or Transferred

### Regulation 9(4) Compliance:
No lifting equipment leaves a business and/or is used, including when received from a third party, unless there is physical evidence that the required thorough examination has been completed.

**Actions Required:**
- ✓ Check for valid examination certificate before transfer
- ✓ Complete handover inspection documentation
- ✓ Transfer all relevant records to new owner/user
- ✓ Notify appropriate parties of any known defects

## 12. Annual Review and Scheme Update

### Review Triggers:

| Trigger | Action Required |
|---|---|
| Annual review | Full scheme assessment |
| Incident or near miss | Immediate scheme revision |
| Change in equipment types | Update examination intervals |
| Regulatory changes | Incorporate new requirements |
| Risk assessment updates | Adjust inspection frequencies |

### Scheme Amendment Process:
1. Review current examination schedule effectiveness
2. Consult with competent persons on findings
3. Update risk assessments based on actual conditions
4. Revise intervals if appropriate by competent person
5. Document all changes and inform relevant personnel
6. Train staff on updated requirements

## 14. Emergency and Exceptional Circumstances

### Events Requiring Additional Examination:
- After extreme weather conditions
- Following impact or collision damage
- After modification or repair that affects safety
- When equipment has been idle for extended period

**Action:** Immediately suspend use and arrange competent person examination.
