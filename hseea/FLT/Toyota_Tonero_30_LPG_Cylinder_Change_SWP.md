## 1. Purpose and Scope

This procedure sets out the safe method for changing the LPG (liquefied petroleum gas / propane) cylinder on the Toyota Tonero 30 forklift truck. It must be followed in full every time a cylinder is changed.

**This procedure applies to:** All designated operators and any person authorised to change LPG cylinders on this vehicle.

## 3. Hazard Summary

> **WARNING: LPG is highly dangerous. Failure to follow this procedure could result in explosion, fire, serious injury, or death.**

LPG (propane) has the following hazardous properties that every operator must understand:

- **Highly pressurised** — leaks extremely easily through any loose or damaged connection
- **Highly flammable and explosive** — requires only a tiny spark to ignite; the explosive range in air is 2–10%
- **Heavier than air** — vapour sinks to floor level and accumulates in low-lying areas, drains, pits, and enclosed spaces
- **Rapid expansion** — one litre of liquid LPG produces approximately 250 litres of vapour
- **Pressure increases with heat** — cylinders must never be stored near heat sources
- **Detectable by smell** — LPG is odourised. If you cannot smell gas but connections were loose, assume gas has already dispersed and the area may still be dangerous
- **Not acutely toxic** — but displaces oxygen in confined spaces and is explosive at very low concentrations
- **No visible flame** — an LPG fire may be invisible in daylight

## 4. Competence Requirements

- **Only trained, designated persons** may change LPG cylinders on this vehicle
- Training must have covered: LPG properties and hazards, this procedure in full, and emergency response
- No unauthorised persons are permitted in the cylinder change area while work is in progress
- If you are unsure of any step, **stop and seek guidance before proceeding**

---

## 5. Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)

All of the following PPE must be worn before beginning any cylinder change:

| PPE Item | Requirement | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| Safety glasses or goggles | Mandatory | Protection from sudden gas release, valve debris, and detergent splashes |
| Leather or heavy-duty work gloves | Mandatory | Protection from cold burns (LPG is cryogenic on release) and mechanical hazards |
| Safety boots with steel toecap | Mandatory | Protection against cylinder drop |
| Clothing | Natural fibres preferred | Synthetic clothing can generate static electricity which may ignite LPG vapour |

---

## 6. Equipment and Materials Required

Before starting, ensure the following are available:

- [ ] Soapy water solution or neutral detergent in a spray bottle *(for leak testing — see Step 23)*
- [ ] Clean cloth or rag *(for wiping detergent from connections after testing)*
- [ ] CO2 or dry powder fire extinguisher *(must be within reach of the work area)*
- [ ] "OUT OF SERVICE" tag *(available if a fault is found)*
- [ ] Cylinder change record sheet *(see Section 11)*

> **NEVER use an open flame, lit match, cigarette, or any spark-producing device for leak detection.**

---

## 7. Cylinder Change Area Requirements

The cylinder change must only take place in a **pre-approved, designated area** that meets all of the following conditions:

- **Well ventilated** — outdoors is strongly preferred. If indoors, the area must have adequate forced mechanical ventilation
- **No ignition sources within 3 metres** — no smoking, no open flames, no running engines, no electrical switches being operated
- **Level, stable surface** — to prevent the FLT and cylinders rolling or falling
- **"FIRE HAZARD — NO SMOKING / NO NAKED FLAMES"** signage posted visibly in the area
- **Drains, pits, and basement access nearby** — note their positions; LPG vapour will flow towards them if a leak occurs
- **CO2 or dry powder fire extinguisher** immediately available
- **First aid provision** accessible nearby

> **Do not change cylinders near parked vehicles, boilers, electrical panels, or any other ignition source.**

---

## 8. Replacement Cylinder Inspection

**Before** bringing a replacement cylinder to the forklift, carry out the following visual inspection:

- [ ] No visible damage, dents, bulging, or significant corrosion on the cylinder body
- [ ] Cylinder is within its pressure test date *(check the date stamp stamped on the collar)*
- [ ] Outflow valve and connection thread are undamaged and free from debris
- [ ] Relief valve safety cap is present and undamaged
- [ ] Cylinder label confirms correct fuel type (propane / LPG)
- [ ] Cylinder valve closed (outflow valve in closed position)

**Do not use any cylinder that fails any part of this inspection.** Label it "DO NOT USE — DEFECTIVE" and segregate it from serviceable stock. Inform your supervisor.

## 9. Step-by-Step Procedure

Work through each phase in the order given. Do not skip or reorder any step.

---

### Phase 1 — FLT Shutdown and Positioning

**Step 1.** Drive the FLT to the designated cylinder change area.

**Step 2.** Apply the parking brake. Lower the forks fully to the ground. Tilt the mast to the vertical position.

**Step 3.** Turn the **LPG tank outflow valve clockwise** until fully closed to shut off the fuel supply to the engine.

**Step 4.** Allow the engine to **run until it stops naturally** — this burns off the residual fuel in the lines. Do not stall or force-stop the engine.

**Step 5.** Once the engine has stopped, turn the **fuel switch to the OFF position**.

**Step 6.** Turn the **ignition switch to OFF** and **remove the key**.

**Step 7.** Allow the engine to cool for a **minimum of 5 minutes** before proceeding.

---

### Phase 2 — Remove the Empty Cylinder

**Step 8.** Turn the **pipe valve clockwise** to close it. *(This valve prevents liquid LPG from spilling when the pipe is disconnected.)*

**Step 9.** Disconnect the piping from the LPG tank by unscrewing the coupling **counter-clockwise**. Stand to the side — do not lean over the connection. Allow any residual pressure to bleed off slowly.

**Step 10.** Pull the **set pin** located at the bottom left of the tank bracket to release it.

**Step 11.** Bring the clamped tank and bracket assembly down toward the **rear of the vehicle**.

**Step 12.** Pull the **tank clamp toward you** to release the band locks.

**Step 13.** Push the bands away and remove the empty cylinder from the bracket.

**Step 14.** Move the empty cylinder **immediately** to the designated empty cylinder storage area. Do not leave it beside the FLT.

---

### Phase 3 — Inspect the Replacement Cylinder

**Step 15.** Carry out the cylinder inspection from Section 8 above, if not already completed.

**Step 16.** Note the **diameter of the replacement cylinder** and confirm the correct band setting will be used:

| Cylinder Diameter | Band Setting |
|---|---|
| 290 mm | Setting 1 |
| 300 mm | Setting 2 |
| 310 mm | Setting 3 |
| 320 mm | Setting 4 |

> Check your cylinder's diameter against the band markings on the bracket. Using the wrong band setting will result in an unsecured cylinder — a serious hazard.

---

### Phase 4 — Install the Replacement Cylinder

**Step 17.** Hook the clamps onto the bands and raise the clamps to the open position.

**Step 18.** Position the replacement cylinder in the bracket. Adjust the band position to match the cylinder's diameter (see Step 16).

**Step 19.** Return the tank bracket toward the **front of the vehicle** until it is fully seated.

**Step 20.** Verify that the **set pin has engaged and locked** securely. Do not proceed if the set pin is not fully engaged.

**Step 21.** Connect the piping to the service valve on the cylinder. Tighten by hand first, then firm up the connection. Do not over-torque; the connection uses a soft metal seal that can be damaged by excessive force.

**Step 22.** Confirm that the **pipe valve is in the open position**.

---

### Phase 5 — Leak Test *(This step is mandatory and must never be skipped)*

> **LPG leaks are invisible and odourless in small concentrations. A leak test must be performed after every cylinder change before the FLT is returned to service.**

**Step 23.** Turn the **LPG tank outflow valve counter-clockwise** to open it fully.

**Step 24.** Verify the pipe valve is open.

**Step 25.** Set the **ignition switch to the ON position** *(do not start the engine)*.

**Step 26.** Turn the **fuel switch ON and OFF repeatedly** several times, then leave it in the **OFF position**. *(This pressurises the fuel system lines.)*

**Step 27.** Apply soapy water or neutral detergent solution generously to the following connections:
- Hose-to-LPG tank connection
- LPG tank outflow valve area
- Regulator inlet connection

**Step 28.** Observe all wetted areas carefully for **bubbles forming in the solution. Bubbles indicate a gas leak.**

**Step 29.** Press the **fuel test bar fitted to the regulator** several times, pushing it toward the outside of the vehicle.

**Step 30.** Apply soapy water to:
- Hose-to-regulator connection
- Regulator-to-carburettor connection

**Step 31.** Observe again for bubbles across all connections.

---

> ### If a leak is detected at any point:
> 1. **Immediately** turn the outflow valve clockwise to close
> 2. Turn ignition OFF
> 3. Do not operate any electrical switches
> 4. Move persons away from the vehicle
> 5. Ventilate the area
> 6. Attach an **"OUT OF SERVICE — DO NOT USE"** tag to the FLT
> 7. **Report immediately to your supervisor**
> 8. Do not attempt repair unless you are a qualified gas engineer
> 9. Do not return the FLT to service until repaired and re-tested by a competent person

---

**Step 32.** If **no leaks are detected**: wipe off **all soapy water and detergent thoroughly** from every connection, fitting, and surface. Residual detergent left on connections can cause corrosion.

---

### Phase 6 — Return to Service

**Step 33.** Wait until there is **no smell of gas** in the area before attempting to start the engine.

**Step 34.** Start the engine in the normal manner as per the FLT operating procedure.

**Step 35.** If the engine fails to start, **do not persist with repeated attempts**. Turn the ignition off, tag the vehicle "OUT OF SERVICE — DO NOT USE", and report for mechanic inspection.

**Step 36.** Complete the **cylinder change record** (Section 11) before the FLT is returned to operational use.

## 10. Cylinder Storage Requirements (DSEAR Compliance)

### Full Cylinders

- Stored **upright** in a designated, secured external cage or compound
- **Minimum 3 metres** from any building opening, doorway, or basement access
- Protected from direct sunlight and all heat sources
- Away from drains, gullies, and any low-lying areas where vapour could accumulate
- **"HIGHLY FLAMMABLE — LPG"** signage prominently displayed
- Cylinder cage/compound to be locked when unattended
- Quantities stored on site must not exceed those assessed in the site DSEAR risk assessment
- Stored separately from oxidising agents, corrosives, and other incompatible materials

### Empty Cylinders

- Stored **separately from full cylinders** and clearly labelled "EMPTY"
- **Treat empty cylinders as if full** — they always retain residual gas and pressure
- The same storage conditions apply as for full cylinders

> Cylinder storage arrangements must be included in the site DSEAR risk assessment. Contact your responsible person if you are uncertain about storage compliance.

## 12. Emergency Procedures

Post this section prominently in the cylinder change area and ensure all operators are familiar with it before being authorised to change cylinders.

---

### Gas Leak Detected

1. **Do not operate any electrical switches, ignition, or lighting** — any spark can ignite LPG vapour
2. Close the **outflow valve** immediately if it is safe to do so without approaching a visible leak
3. Move **all persons away** from the area — remember LPG vapour sinks and will accumulate at floor level
4. **Ventilate the area** — open doors and windows manually (do not operate electrical switches to activate powered ventilation)
5. Eliminate all ignition sources: smoking, mobile phones, running engines nearby
6. If the gas smell persists, the source cannot be isolated, or a large release has occurred: **evacuate the building/area and call 999**
7. **Do not re-enter** the affected area until it has been declared safe by a competent person

---

### Fire Involving LPG

1. **Raise the fire alarm immediately**
2. **Evacuate** all persons from the immediate area
3. Use a **CO2 or dry powder extinguisher** — aim at the base of the flame
4. **Never use water** on an LPG fire — it will not extinguish it and may cause a violent reaction
5. If the cylinder itself is involved in the fire and cannot be extinguished safely, evacuate to a minimum of **50 metres** and keep others away
6. **Call 999** — inform them that LPG cylinders are involved and give the location
7. **Do not attempt to move a burning cylinder**
8. Await arrival of the emergency services and provide them with information about the quantity and location of cylinders on site

## 13. Out of Service Criteria

The FLT must be tagged **"OUT OF SERVICE — DO NOT USE"** and removed from service if any of the following apply:

- Any gas leak is detected that cannot be immediately resolved by tightening a connection
- Any hose, pipe, or fitting shows signs of damage, cracking, abrasion, or deterioration
- The regulator is damaged, leaking, or not functioning correctly
- The cylinder is physically damaged, corroded, or past its pressure test date
- The vehicle smells of gas after cylinder change and after an engine start attempt
- Any safety device (relief valve, pipe valve, set pin) is missing or damaged
- The engine fails to start following a cylinder change

The vehicle must not be returned to service until inspected and cleared by a competent person.

---

## 14. Do Not / Prohibited Actions

The following actions are **strictly prohibited** at all times:

- **No smoking** anywhere in the cylinder change area or cylinder storage area
- **No open flames, matches, lighters** in the cylinder change area
- **No open flame leak testing** — soapy water or detergent only
- **Do not park the FLT** in direct sunlight or near heat sources when it has an LPG cylinder fitted
- **Do not store cylinders** on their sides, near heat sources, or in confined, poorly ventilated spaces
- **Do not use a damaged cylinder** — remove from service immediately
- **Do not attempt mechanical repairs** to the LPG fuel system unless you are a qualified gas engineer
- **Do not attempt to refill cylinders on site** — cylinders must only be refilled by the supplier

## 15. Regulator Maintenance Note

*(For reference — this task must only be carried out by authorised maintenance personnel.)*

Tar and residue can accumulate in the regulator. The manual specifies this should be checked weekly:

1. Allow the engine to cool before servicing
2. Connect a hose to the drain cock located underneath the regulator
3. Place an oil pan below to collect waste
4. Open the drain cock and allow tar to drain into the pan
5. Close the drain cock and disconnect the hose
6. Wipe off any tar adhering to the vehicle

Report excessive tar accumulation to a qualified engineer.
