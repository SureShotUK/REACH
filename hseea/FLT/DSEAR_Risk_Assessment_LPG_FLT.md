## 1. Purpose and Scope

This document is the DSEAR risk assessment required by **Regulation 5 of the Dangerous Substances and Explosive Atmospheres Regulations 2002 (SI 2002/2776)**. It covers all activities involving LPG/propane as a fuel for forklift trucks (FLTs) on site, including:

- Normal FLT operation in the warehouse and between buildings
- LPG cylinder changing
- LPG cylinder storage

It determines whether explosive atmospheres may occur at any location, classifies hazardous zones where they may, and identifies the controls required to ensure safety.

## 3. Dangerous Substance Identification

### Substance: Propane (LPG)

| Property | Value |
|---|---|
| Chemical name | Propane (C₃H₈) |
| Common name | LPG (Liquefied Petroleum Gas) |
| Form at point of use | Liquefied gas in pressurised cylinder; vapour in fuel lines |
| Lower Explosive Limit (LEL) | **2.1% v/v in air** |
| Upper Explosive Limit (UEL) | **9.5% v/v in air** |
| Flash point | –104°C (always flammable at ambient temperatures) |
| Auto-ignition temperature | ~470°C |
| Vapour density relative to air | ~1.5 — **heavier than air; sinks and accumulates at floor level and in drains/pits** |
| Odour | Added odorant (ethyl mercaptan) — detectable at ~0.4% v/v (well below LEL) |
| Toxicity | Not acutely toxic; asphyxiant in confined spaces at high concentrations |
| SDS reference | Obtain from LPG supplier and keep on site |

> **Key hazard**: Any mixture of propane vapour with air between 2.1% and 9.5% v/v constitutes an **explosive atmosphere** under DSEAR. A single spark or ignition source is sufficient to cause ignition.

### Quantities on Site

| Item | Cylinder size | Maximum number on site | Maximum LPG on site |
|---|---|---|---|
| Cylinders fitted to FLTs | 18 kg | 2 (one per FLT) | 36 kg |
| Spare/full cylinders in storage | 18 kg | Estimated 2–4 | 36–72 kg |
| **Total maximum** | | | **~108 kg** |

**COMAH threshold (lower tier): 50 tonnes** — not triggered. No notification to HSE required under COMAH.

**BCGA CP7 quantity band: 15–400 kg** — standard storage arrangements apply (see Section 10).

**Fire authority notification threshold: ~400 kg** — not triggered at current quantities.

## 5. Risk Assessment (Regulation 5)

### Likelihood and Consequence Definitions

**Likelihood:**
| Rating | Definition |
|---|---|
| Rare | Release of LPG could only occur due to equipment failure or major abnormal event |
| Unlikely | Release possible in abnormal or fault conditions; not expected in normal operation |
| Possible | Release may occur during normal operations (e.g., during cylinder disconnection) |

**Consequence (if explosive atmosphere ignites):**
| Rating | Definition |
|---|---|
| Minor | First aid injury; no structural damage |
| Serious | Lost time injury; localised fire or minor explosion |
| Catastrophic | Fatality or multiple casualties; major structural damage |

> Note: The consequence of any LPG ignition is inherently **catastrophic** due to the explosive energy of propane. Controls must focus on preventing explosive atmosphere formation and eliminating ignition sources.

---

### Activity A — Normal FLT Operation

| Assessment element | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Description** | Toyota Tonero 30 operating on warehouse floor; travel between warehouses; loading/unloading at bay |
| **LPG release mechanism** | Sealed LPG fuel system: tank, regulator, solenoid valve, carburettor. Release only possible via: mechanical damage, hose failure, loose connection, or regulator fault |
| **Explosive atmosphere potential** | Very low in normal operation. The FLT fuel system is a closed pressure system — no deliberate release occurs during operation |
| **Likelihood of explosive atmosphere** | **Rare** (sealed system; release requires fault condition) |
| **Consequence if ignition** | **Catastrophic** |
| **Pre-controls risk** | High |
| **Controls in place** | (1) Operator pre-use inspection of fuel system daily; (2) Periodic maintenance and inspection of LPG fuel system by competent engineer; (3) No smoking policy across all operating areas; (4) No naked flames policy; (5) SWP-FLT-LPG-001 — operators trained; (6) Regulator tar drain carried out as per manufacturer schedule (weekly) |
| **Residual risk** | **Low** |
| **Zone classification — general warehouse floor** | **Unclassified** — no explosive atmosphere expected in normal operation |
| **Zone classification — within ~1m of FLT fuel system connections/regulator** | **Zone 2** — abnormal release could occur at these points; treat as Zone 2 for inspection and maintenance operations |

---

### Activity B — LPG Cylinder Changing

| Assessment element | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Description** | Designated operator changes empty LPG cylinder for a full replacement cylinder following SWP-FLT-LPG-001 |
| **LPG release mechanism** | Deliberate disconnection and reconnection of the fuel pipe to the cylinder service valve. Even when pipe valve is closed first (per SWP Step 8), a very small residual release on disconnection is possible. Brief vapour release at the connection point |
| **Explosive atmosphere potential** | Possible but limited — any released vapour is a small volume and disperses rapidly in the well-ventilated warehouse (large drive-through doors) |
| **Likelihood of explosive atmosphere reaching LEL** | **Unlikely** — large airflow from open warehouse doors prevents accumulation; release is brief and small-volume |
| **Consequence if ignition** | **Catastrophic** |
| **Pre-controls risk** | High |
| **Controls in place** | (1) SWP-FLT-LPG-001 followed in full — outflow valve closed before disconnection; pipe valve closed; ignition off; key removed; (2) Cylinder change only in designated area with warehouse doors fully open; (3) No ignition sources within 3m during change; no smoking, no running engines nearby, no electrical switching; (4) "FIRE HAZARD — NO SMOKING / NO NAKED FLAMES" sign posted at change area; (5) Leak test (soapy water / fuel test bar) before engine restart; (6) Engine not started until no smell of gas; (7) CO2 or dry powder extinguisher immediately available; (8) Only trained designated operators carry out the change |
| **Residual risk** | **Low** |
| **Zone classification — during cylinder change operation** | **Zone 2** within approximately 1 metre radius of the cylinder connections — applies during the change operation only. This zone collapses to unclassified once the leak test has passed and the area has been cleared |
| **Zone classification — cylinder change area at other times** | **Unclassified** — no cylinders present; large ventilation prevents accumulation |

---

### Activity C — LPG Cylinder Storage

| Assessment element | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Description** | Full and empty cylinders stored in an outdoor secured cage adjacent to the warehouse |
| **LPG release mechanism** | Valve failure, physical damage to cylinder, or relief valve operation in high temperature. All cylinders stored with valves closed |
| **Explosive atmosphere potential** | Very low — outdoor location means any release disperses freely. No confinement |
| **Likelihood of explosive atmosphere reaching LEL** | **Rare** — outdoor storage; release only on fault condition; vapour cannot accumulate |
| **Consequence if ignition** | **Catastrophic** |
| **Pre-controls risk** | Medium |
| **Controls in place** | (1) Outdoor secured cage — free air ventilation on all sides; (2) Cylinders stored upright, valves closed; (3) Empty and full cylinders stored separately and labelled; (4) Cage located away from building openings, drains, and ignition sources; (5) "HIGHLY FLAMMABLE LPG — NO SMOKING — NO NAKED FLAMES" signage on cage; (6) Cage locked when unattended; (7) Protected from direct sunlight and heat sources; (8) Pre-use inspection of cylinders before installation (per SWP-FLT-LPG-001 Section 8) |
| **Residual risk** | **Low** |
| **Zone classification** | **Zone 2** within approximately 1 metre of the cage on all sides — this is a permanent Zone 2 area. The area beyond 1 metre from the cage is **unclassified** |

## 6. Zone Classification Summary (Regulation 7 and Schedule 2)

The following table summarises the zone classification for all relevant areas on site. A simple site sketch should be attached to this document or produced separately, showing the zone extents to scale.

| Location | Zone | Extent | Applicable at |
|---|---|---|---|
| General warehouse floor | **Unclassified** | Entire floor area | Always |
| Within ~1m of FLT fuel system during maintenance/inspection | **Zone 2** | 1m radius of connections | During maintenance operations only |
| Cylinder change area — during change operation | **Zone 2** | 1m radius of connection point | During cylinder change only |
| Cylinder change area — at all other times | **Unclassified** | — | When not in use |
| Outdoor cylinder storage cage — within ~1m of cage | **Zone 2** | 1m around the cage perimeter | Always (permanent Zone 2) |
| Beyond 1m from storage cage | **Unclassified** | — | Always |

### Zone 2 Implications

In any **Zone 2** area:
- All permanently installed electrical equipment must be **ATEX Category 3G** rated (or better)
- Portable equipment (mobile phones, radios, power tools) must be intrinsically safe or must be switched off and not used within the Zone 2 boundary
- No ignition sources — no smoking, no open flames, no spark-producing activities
- Signage: **"HIGHLY FLAMMABLE — NO SMOKING — NO NAKED FLAMES"**

> **Note on the outdoor storage cage:** Check whether any permanently installed electrical equipment (lighting, security cameras, alarm contacts) is within the Zone 2 boundary of the storage cage. If so, it must be ATEX Cat 3G rated. If not, no specific ATEX equipment is required in this area.

## 7. Ignition Source Assessment (Regulation 6)

The following ignition sources have been assessed for all Zone 2 areas:

| Ignition source | Present? | Control measure |
|---|---|---|
| Naked flames | Prohibited near FLT and storage | No smoking / no naked flames policy; signage; operator training |
| Smoking | Prohibited in all work areas | Site no-smoking policy; signage |
| Electrical sparks (switching) | Possible from electrical equipment | No electrical switching within Zone 2 during cylinder change; ATEX-rated equipment in permanent Zone 2 areas |
| Mechanical sparks (metal-on-metal) | Low risk — cylinder fittings are brass/soft metal | Non-sparking tools used for connections; no steel tools struck near cylinders |
| Hot surfaces (engine, exhaust) | Present on FLT | Ignition key removed before cylinder change; engine cooled before work (SWP Step 7); no cylinder change near running vehicles |
| Static electricity | Low risk in this context | Operators earthed via normal footwear; cylinders handled on ground |
| Mobile phones / radios | Present — operators may carry them | Operators instructed to switch off or not use phones/radios within Zone 2 during cylinder change |

## 8. Equipment Assessment (Regulation 8)

For permanently installed electrical equipment in the **permanent Zone 2 area** (within ~1m of the outdoor cylinder storage cage):

- Any lighting, security cameras, PIR sensors, or other electrical installations within this boundary must be **ATEX Category 3G** (Zone 2 rated) or positioned outside the 1m boundary
- Action: carry out a check of all installed electrical equipment in and around the storage cage area and confirm ATEX ratings or relocate equipment outside the zone

For the **temporary Zone 2 area** (cylinder change area, active only during the change operation):
- No permanently installed electrical equipment should be in the designated cylinder change area
- If lighting is required, use ATEX-rated portable lighting or ensure the area is naturally lit

## 10. Controls Summary (Regulation 6)

Controls are applied following the hierarchy required by DSEAR Reg 6:

| Hierarchy level | Control applied |
|---|---|
| **Elimination** | Not reasonably practicable — LPG is the operational fuel for existing FLT fleet. Electric FLTs are an alternative for consideration at fleet renewal |
| **Substitution** | Not applied at this time |
| **Reduce quantity to minimum** | Minimum cylinder stock maintained — cylinders ordered and delivered as needed; no excess stock held beyond operational requirements |
| **Engineering controls** | Natural ventilation via large warehouse doors (drive-through scale) prevents vapour accumulation; outdoor storage cage prevents vapour confinement |
| **Administrative controls** | SWP-FLT-LPG-001 (cylinder change procedure); designated change area; no smoking / no naked flames policy; trained designated operators only; pre-use inspection; leak testing |
| **PPE** | Safety glasses, leather gloves, steel toecap boots (per SWP-FLT-LPG-001 Section 5) |

## 11. Emergency Arrangements (Regulation 9)

Emergency procedures are documented in **SWP-FLT-LPG-001, Section 12** and must be:

- Posted prominently in the designated cylinder change area
- Covered during operator training
- Reviewed annually or following any incident

**Summary of emergency actions** (full detail in SWP-FLT-LPG-001 Section 12):

- **Gas leak**: Close outflow valve; do not operate electrical switches; evacuate area; ventilate; call 999 if not controlled
- **Fire involving LPG**: Raise alarm; evacuate; CO2 or dry powder extinguisher only (never water); evacuate to 50m if cylinder involved; call 999

**Fire extinguisher provision**: A CO2 or dry powder extinguisher must be available immediately at the cylinder change area and within reasonable reach of the storage cage. Check and record monthly.

## 14. Conclusions

1. Propane/LPG is a dangerous substance under DSEAR 2002. This risk assessment satisfies the duty under **Regulation 5**.

2. The general warehouse floor is assessed as **unclassified** under DSEAR — the sealed FLT fuel system does not release LPG in normal operation, and the large-door natural ventilation prevents accumulation if an abnormal release were to occur.

3. **Zone 2** classification applies: within ~1m of the outdoor cylinder storage cage (permanent), and within ~1m of cylinder connections during the change operation (temporary).

4. The outdoor cylinder storage arrangement satisfies **BCGA CP7** standard requirements for the 15–400 kg quantity band. Total on-site LPG (~108 kg maximum) is well below COMAH and fire authority notification thresholds.

5. **SWP-FLT-LPG-001** satisfies the **Regulation 10** (safe system of work) requirement. Adherence to the SWP is the key administrative control and must not be treated as optional.

6. Residual risk for all three activities is assessed as **Low** with controls in place.

7. **ATEX equipment check**: A check of permanently installed electrical equipment within the Zone 2 boundary of the outdoor storage cage is required — see Section 8.
