# Risk Assessment Best Practices - Gemini CLI Guidelines

This document outlines the best practices for conducting risk assessments, aligning with the guidelines provided by the Health and Safety Executive (HSE) in the UK. This guidance is intended to ensure that all risk assessments are thorough, legally compliant, and effectively contribute to a safer working environment.

## HSE's Five Steps to Risk Assessment

The HSE advocates a clear, systematic approach to risk assessment, broken down into five key steps. Following these steps helps to identify hazards, assess risks, and implement effective control measures.

1.  **Identify hazards:**
    *   What activities, processes, or substances in the workplace could cause harm?
    *   Consider physical, mental, chemical, and biological hazards.
    *   Examples: machinery, slips/trips, stress, asbestos, infectious diseases.

2.  **Decide who may be harmed, and how:**
    *   Who might be at risk (employees, visitors, contractors, public)?
    *   How could they be harmed by each identified hazard?
    *   Consider vulnerable groups (e.g., pregnant workers, young persons).

3.  **Assess the risks and take action/control the risks:**
    *   Evaluate the likelihood of harm occurring and the potential severity.
    *   Implement control measures to eliminate or reduce risks "as far as reasonably practicable."
    *   Prioritize actions based on the severity and likelihood of the risk.
    *   Follow the hierarchy of control: Eliminate, Substitute, Engineering Controls, Administrative Controls, Personal Protective Equipment (PPE).

4.  **Record your findings:**
    *   If you have five or more employees, legally you *must* record significant findings.
    *   Document identified hazards, those at risk, and the control measures in place.
    *   This record demonstrates that a proper assessment was made and precautions are reasonable.

5.  **Review the risk assessment:**
    *   Risk assessments are not static; they must be reviewed regularly.
    *   Review if there are changes in the workplace, new equipment, new processes, or after an incident.
    *   Ensure the assessment remains effective and up-to-date.

## Managing for Health and Safety (HSG65) - Plan, Do, Check, Act

HSG65 provides a framework for effective health and safety management, emphasizing a "Plan, Do, Check, Act" (PDCA) approach for continuous improvement.

*   **Plan:** Develop policy, set objectives, define responsibilities, plan risk identification and assessment.
*   **Do:** Implement the plan, organize activities, communicate, provide resources, implement preventative measures.
*   **Check:** Monitor performance through inspections and audits, looking at both leading and lagging indicators.
*   **Act:** Review performance, learn from incidents, and continuously improve the health and safety management system.

## Risk Assessment Best Practice Checklist

Use this checklist to evaluate whether a risk assessment conforms to best practices and HSE guidelines.

### Section 1: Core Principles & Legal Compliance

- [ ] **1.1 Identification of Hazards:** Are all significant hazards (physical, mental, chemical, biological) clearly identified and described?
- [ ] **1.2 Who is at Risk:** Is it clear who might be harmed by each hazard (employees, contractors, visitors, public) and how? Are vulnerable groups considered?
- [ ] **1.3 Risk Evaluation:** Is there a clear assessment of the likelihood and severity of harm for each risk?
- [ ] **1.4 Control Measures:** Are specific, practical control measures identified to eliminate or reduce the risks?
- [ ] **1.5 Hierarchy of Control:** Do the control measures follow the hierarchy of control (Eliminate, Substitute, Engineering, Administrative, PPE)?
- [ ] **1.6 Reasonably Practicable:** Have all measures been taken "as far as reasonably practicable" to control risks?
- [ ] **1.7 Documentation (5+ Employees):** If applicable (5 or more employees), are the significant findings of the risk assessment recorded? This includes hazards, those at risk, and control measures.
- [ ] **1.8 Review Periodicity:** Is there a defined review period or triggers for review (e.g., change in process, new equipment, incident)?

### Section 2: Content & Thoroughness

- [ ] **2.1 Scope Definition:** Is the scope of the risk assessment clearly defined (e.g., specific activity, area, equipment)?
- [ ] **2.2 Activity Description:** Is the activity or task being assessed clearly and accurately described?
- [ ] **2.3 Existing Controls:** Are any existing control measures clearly documented?
- [ ] **2.4 Additional Actions:** Are any additional actions required to further reduce risk clearly listed, with responsible persons and target dates?
- [ ] **2.5 Training Requirements:** Are any specific training requirements related to the identified risks or control measures noted?
- [ ] **2.6 Emergency Procedures:** Where applicable, does the risk assessment consider emergency procedures related to the hazards?
- [ ] **2.7 Consultation:** Is there evidence that employees or their representatives were consulted in the development of the risk assessment?

### Section 3: Review & Maintenance

- [ ] **3.1 Date of Assessment:** Is the date the risk assessment was conducted clearly stated?
- [ ] **3.2 Assessor Details:** Is the name and position of the person(s) who conducted the assessment recorded?
- [ ] **3.3 Date of Review:** Is the date of the last review clearly stated?
- [ ] **3.4 Effectiveness of Controls:** Does the review process confirm the effectiveness of existing control measures?
- [ ] **3.5 Updated as Necessary:** Has the risk assessment been updated following any changes or incidents?

This checklist provides a robust framework to ensure that risk assessments are comprehensive, compliant, and continuously improved, fostering a proactive approach to health and safety management.
