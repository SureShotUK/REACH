## 1. Purpose and Scope

This policy sets out the requirements for managing visitors and contractors attending Portland Fuel's office premises, with particular emphasis on fire safety. It applies to all visitors, including:

- Business visitors (clients, suppliers, partners)
- Contractors carrying out maintenance, installation, or inspection work
- Delivery personnel remaining on site for more than a brief visit

All visitors and contractors must comply with this policy. The member of staff hosting or arranging the visit is responsible for ensuring compliance.

## 3. Pre-Visit Requirements

### 3.1 Language and Communication

Before any visit is confirmed, the host must establish whether the visitor or contractor has difficulty speaking or understanding English. If a communication barrier exists:

- The host must notify the fire marshal team in advance of the visit.
- A translation of the site fire emergency plan must be obtained in the visitor's primary language before they arrive on site.
- The fire emergency plan translation must be provided to the visitor on arrival and its contents explained (using a translator or translation aid if necessary).
- The visitor must not be left unaccompanied until this briefing has been completed.

### 3.2 Disabilities and Mobility Issues

Before any visit is confirmed, the host must ask the visitor whether they have any disability, mobility impairment, or health condition that could affect their ability to evacuate the building unaided in an emergency. This includes but is not limited to:

- Difficulty walking, using stairs, or moving quickly
- Use of a wheelchair, crutches, walking frame, or other mobility aid
- Visual or hearing impairment
- Any temporary condition (e.g. post-operative recovery, injury)

Where a mobility or evacuation concern is identified:

1. The host must inform the fire marshal team before the visit.
2. A **Personal Emergency Evacuation Plan (PEEP)** must be completed for that individual before or on arrival.
3. The PEEP must identify: the nature of the impairment, the evacuation route to be used, the assistance required, and the name of the person responsible for assisting.
4. The fire marshal team must be briefed on the PEEP and confirm they can support it.
5. The visitor must not begin their visit until the PEEP is in place and understood by all parties.

## 4. Visitor Sign-In and Sign-Out

### 4.1 Sign-In

All visitors and contractors must sign in at reception on arrival. The sign-in record must capture:

- Full name
- Company (if applicable)
- Name of host / Portland Fuel contact
- Purpose of visit
- Time of arrival
- Vehicle registration (if applicable)

### 4.2 Sign-Out

Before leaving the site, all visitors and contractors must:

1. Notify their host directly that they are leaving.
2. Sign out at reception, recording their time of departure.
3. The host must then inform the fire marshal team that the visitor has left site.

This ensures the fire marshal team always has an accurate picture of who is present on site.

---

## 5. Lone Contractors

Where a contractor arrives on site unaccompanied (i.e. as a single person, without colleagues):

1. The host must inform the **fire marshal team** before the contractor begins work, providing:
   - The contractor's name
   - The location within the building where they will be working
   - The time of arrival
   - The expected time of departure
2. The contractor must be briefed on the fire escape policy (see Section 7) before starting work.
3. If the contractor changes location within the building, they must inform their host, who must update the fire marshal team.
4. On departure, the host must confirm to the fire marshal team that the lone contractor has left site and is no longer present.

The fire marshal team must maintain a log of lone contractors on site at any given time.

## 6. Fire Safety Induction on Arrival

Before any visitor or contractor begins their visit or work, the host must conduct a brief verbal fire safety induction covering:

- Location of the nearest emergency exit(s) to their area
- Location of the muster point: **[MUSTER POINT]**
- What to do on hearing the fire alarm (stop work immediately, leave via the nearest exit, proceed to the muster point, do not use lifts, do not re-enter the building)
- Whether a fire alarm test is scheduled for that day (see Section 7)
- Who to report to at the muster point

The host is responsible for ensuring this induction takes place and must not leave a visitor unescorted until it has been completed.

---

## 7. Planned Fire Alarm Tests

A **weekly fire alarm test** is conducted every **Friday at 15:55 (3:55 PM)**. This is a test only and does not require evacuation.

All visitors and contractors present on site at that time must be informed **before the alarm sounds** that it is a scheduled test. The host is responsible for communicating this.

If a full evacuation drill is planned on the day of a visit, the host must inform the visitor in advance and ensure they participate in the drill.

---

## 8. Emergency Exits and Evacuation Routes

On arrival, the host must point out to the visitor:

- The location of all emergency exits accessible from the areas they will be using
- The primary evacuation route from their work area
- Any areas that must not be used during evacuation (e.g. service lifts)
- The muster point: **[MUSTER POINT]**

Visitors and contractors must not:

- Prop open or obstruct fire doors at any time
- Block or store materials in emergency escape corridors or stairwells
- Lock or wedge shut any fire exit

## 9. Hot Work Permit

Any contractor wishing to carry out **hot work** on the premises must obtain a **Hot Work Permit to Work** before any such work begins. Hot work includes, but is not limited to:

- Welding (MIG, TIG, arc)
- Brazing and soldering
- Cutting with an angle grinder or disc cutter
- Use of a blowtorch or heat gun
- Any other activity producing a naked flame, sparks, or significant heat

**The Hot Work Permit to Work is issued by:**

| James Spencer | james@portland-fuel.co.uk |

No hot work may commence until the signed permit has been received. The permit will specify:

- The exact location and nature of the work
- Start and end time of the work
- Required fire precautions (e.g. removal of combustibles, fire watch, fire extinguisher standby)
- Post-work inspection requirements

The contractor is responsible for complying with all conditions stated in the permit. The permit must be kept on site during the hot work and returned to the issuer on completion.

## 10. General Conduct on Site

All visitors and contractors must:

- Remain in the areas relevant to their visit or work
- Not enter plant rooms, server rooms, or restricted areas without express permission
- Follow any reasonable instruction given by Portland Fuel staff, including the fire marshal team
- Report any fire hazard, fire, or smoke immediately to their host or any member of staff
- Not interfere with fire detection or suppression equipment
- Not smoke except in designated external areas

---

## 12. Non-Compliance

Failure by a contractor or visitor to comply with this policy — including commencing hot work without a permit, obstructing fire exits, or failing to sign in — will result in the visit being terminated immediately. The contractor or visitor will be required to leave the premises.

Repeated or serious non-compliance by a contractor company may result in that company being removed from Portland Fuel's approved contractor list.

---

## 13. Document Review

This policy will be reviewed annually, or following:

- A fire incident or near-miss on site
- A change in site layout or emergency arrangements
- An update to relevant legislation or HSE guidance
- Feedback from a fire safety audit or inspection
