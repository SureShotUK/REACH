## How the Unit Works

The MSZ-AP60VGK is a **recirculation-only** unit. It draws air from within the office, passes it across the heat exchanger (to cool or heat), and returns it to the room. **It does not introduce fresh outdoor air.** There is no outdoor air intake on either the indoor or outdoor unit.

This means:
- **CO₂ and humidity** in the office are unaffected by the air conditioner — separate ventilation (windows, mechanical fresh-air supply) is required to manage indoor air quality.
- The unit will condition and filter whatever air is already in the room, but it will not dilute occupant-generated pollutants or odours with fresh air.

## Filtration Capability

The AP series uses a **two-stage filtration** system:

### Stage 1 — Standard Washable Dust Filter

- Coarse mesh filter positioned across the air intake.
- Captures dust, lint, and larger airborne particles before they reach the heat exchanger.
- Washable and reusable (part number MAC-2360FT-E).
- **Not rated to HEPA standard** and has no published MERV efficiency rating — it is a mechanical pre-filter, not a fine-particle or respiratory-grade filter.

### Stage 2 — Anti-Allergy Enzyme Filter

- Fitted as standard on the AP series.
- Uses an enzyme coating to capture and biologically deactivate allergens including mould spores, pollen, and dust mite faecal matter.
- Reduces the recirculation of deactivated allergen fragments back into the room.
- This filter does **not** capture fine particulate matter (PM2.5), viruses, bacteria, or volatile organic compounds (VOCs) — it is an allergen-deactivation filter, not a HEPA or activated carbon filter.

### What the Filtration Does Not Cover

| Contaminant | Addressed? |
|------------|-----------|
| Large dust particles | Yes (Stage 1) |
| Pollen / mould spores | Partially (Stage 2 deactivates, does not remove) |
| Fine particulate (PM2.5) | No |
| Bacteria / viruses | No |
| VOCs / chemical odours | No |
| CO₂ / fresh air supply | No |

## Filter Maintenance

Mitsubishi Electric recommends cleaning the **dust filter every two weeks** under normal conditions, and more frequently in dusty environments.

**Cleaning procedure (dust filter):**
1. Switch the unit off and remove the front panel.
2. Slide out the filter.
3. Vacuum loose dust, then rinse with lukewarm water.
4. Allow to dry completely before refitting — do not refit a wet filter.
5. Refit and close the front panel.

The anti-allergy enzyme filter should be replaced periodically; refer to the operating manual for the recommended replacement interval.
