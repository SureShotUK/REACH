# Fire Alarm System Servicing: Small Office — Frequency & Requirements

**Premises type:** Small office, ~20 admin staff, low-risk occupancy  
**Applicable standard:** BS 5839-1:2025  
**Legal duty:** <a href="https://www.legislation.gov.uk/uksi/2005/1541/contents/made" target="_blank">Regulatory Reform (Fire Safety) Order 2005</a>, Article 17

---

## Legal Duty Summary

The **Responsible Person** (typically the employer or building owner) is legally required under the
Regulatory Reform (Fire Safety) Order 2005 to ensure fire detection and alarm systems are properly
maintained. BS 5839-1 is the British Standard that defines what "properly maintained" means in practice.
Failure to comply can result in prosecution, fines, and invalidated insurance.

---

## Required Servicing Frequency

| Visit type | Who carries it out | Minimum frequency |
|---|---|---|
| Inspection & service | External competent person / engineer | **Twice per year (six-monthly)** |
| Weekly alarm test | In-house staff (Responsible Person or delegate) | Weekly |
| Visual panel check | In-house staff | Monthly |

**Annual servicing alone is not compliant.** BS 5839-1 requires a minimum of two inspection and
test visits per year, spaced approximately six months apart. The 2025 revision introduced a
permitted scheduling window of **5 to 7 months** between visits — no visit should be earlier than
5 months or later than 7 months from the previous one.

For a small, low-risk office this means **two professional service visits per year** is the
appropriate standard. Quarterly servicing is generally recommended only for higher-risk premises
(care homes, hospitals, dusty/industrial environments, or systems with a high false alarm rate).

---

## What the External Competent Person Must Do

Each six-monthly visit must work towards **100% functional coverage of all system devices across
the two visits combined** over any 12-month period. Typically split so that approximately half the
devices are fully tested at each visit.

**At each visit the engineer should:**

- Inspect and test a rotating sample of manual call points
- Inspect and test a rotating sample of automatic detectors (smoke, heat)
- Test all audible and visual alarm devices (sounders, beacons)
- Check the control and indicating equipment (panel, zone LEDs, fault indicators)
- Test mains power supply and standby battery condition
- Inspect all visible cabling for damage or deterioration
- Check any remote alarm signalling or monitoring links
- Review and update the fire alarm log book
- Issue a written service report detailing all findings, any deficiencies, and recommended actions

---

## Who Qualifies as a Competent Person

BS 5839-1 recommends the servicing engineer be from a company holding **third-party certification**
from a UKAS-accredited body. In practice this means:

- **BAFE SP203-1** registered companies — the most widely recognised scheme for fire detection and
  alarm system maintenance in the UK
- Engineers with appropriate manufacturer training and up-to-date competency

Using a non-certified contractor does not automatically mean non-compliance, but a BAFE-registered
company provides the clearest evidence of competence if challenged by the fire authority or insurer.

---

## In-House Routine Checks (No External Engineer Required)

These must be carried out by the Responsible Person or a designated member of staff and logged:

| Check | Frequency | What to do |
|---|---|---|
| Alarm function test | Weekly | Operate one manual call point; verify alarm sounds throughout; rotate to a different call point each week |
| Control panel inspection | Monthly | Check for fault or warning indicators on the panel; log any issues |
| Post-alarm check | After any activation | Log the event, identify cause, reset panel |

---

## Record Keeping

All tests, inspections, and service visits must be recorded in the **fire alarm log book**
(kept on site). The log book should contain:

- Date and result of every weekly test
- Date, scope, findings, and engineer's name for every professional service visit
- Any faults identified and the date they were rectified
- Any changes or additions to the system

Records must be available for inspection by the fire authority on request.

---

## Summary for a Small Low-Risk Office

> A small office with 20 admin staff requires a professional inspection and service by a competent
> external engineer **twice a year**, with visits spaced 5–7 months apart. In-house staff should
> carry out a weekly manual call point test. There is no provision in BS 5839-1 or the RRO 2005
> for small or low-risk offices to reduce this to annual-only professional servicing.
