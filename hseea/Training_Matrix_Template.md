# Training Matrix Template
## AdBlue Manufacturing Site

---

## How to Use This Template

**Purpose:** Track training status for all employees and visiting personnel, ensuring compliance with legal requirements and renewal dates are managed proactively.

**Instructions:**
1. **Part 1 - Training Requirements Reference:** Lists all training types, frequencies, who needs them, and legal requirements
2. **Part 2 - Individual Staff Training Matrix:** Tracks each person's training status, dates, and renewals
3. **Part 3 - Training Schedule Planner:** Forward-looking schedule of upcoming training needs
4. **Part 4 - Training Provider Contacts:** Maintain list of approved training providers

**Updating the Matrix:**
- Review monthly at minimum
- Update immediately when training is completed
- Flag renewals 3 months before expiry
- Annual review in Q4 to plan next year's training budget

| **Confined Space Awareness** | Confined Spaces Regs 1997 | Initial + refresher every 3 years | Only if entering confined spaces (tanks, vessels) | £200-300/person | Only if applicable to your operations |
| **Working at Height** | Work at Height Regs 2005 | Initial + refresher every 2-3 years | Anyone using ladders >2m, working on racking | £150-200/person | If accessing high racking or roof areas |
| **Abrasive Wheels** | PUWER 1998 | Initial + refresher every 3-5 years | Only if using angle grinders or similar | £100-150/person | Only if applicable |
| **Spillage Response** | Environmental Protection Act 1990 | Initial + refresher every 2 years | Warehouse operatives, emergency response team | £0-200 (internal or external) | Specific to urea/AdBlue spills |

## Tips for Effective Training Management

1. **Set calendar reminders** for 3 months before expiry dates to allow booking time
2. **Batch training where possible** to reduce costs (e.g., manual handling + COSHH in one session)
3. **Keep physical or scanned copies** of all training certificates (retention: duration of employment + 6 years)
4. **Document internal training** (toolbox talks, inductions) with date, topic, attendees, trainer signature
5. **Annual budget planning** in Q4: review matrix and estimate next year's training costs
6. **New starter checklist:** Ensure immediate induction, then role-specific training within 1 month
7. **Ask for proof of training** from contractors/agency staff before allowing work to commence
8. **Link training to job descriptions** so recruitment specifies required qualifications
9. **Include training in performance reviews** to demonstrate commitment to competence
10. **Celebrate training achievements** to encourage a positive learning culture

## Common Questions

**Q: How long must we keep training records?**
A: Duration of employment + 6 years minimum. Some specific training (e.g., asbestos awareness, health surveillance) requires 40-year retention.

**Q: Can we deliver training internally?**
A: Yes, for HSE induction, toolbox talks, and refresher training on internal procedures. For accredited training (forklift, first aid, fire warden), you must use an accredited external provider.

**Q: What if training expires while someone is on long-term sick leave?**
A: They cannot perform the role requiring that training until it's renewed. Arrange refresher training as part of return-to-work planning.

**Q: Do we need to train temporary staff?**
A: Yes. All temporary and agency staff must receive site induction and role-specific training before starting work. Check with agency what training they've already provided.

**Q: What training do visiting drivers need?**
A: Minimum: site safety induction (speed limits, routes, loading procedures, emergency procedures). Check they have valid driving license and Driver CPC if HGV. Keep records of inductions.

**Q: Can online training replace classroom training?**
A: For some topics, yes (DSE, general H&S awareness). For practical skills (forklift, first aid, fire extinguisher use), classroom/practical training is required.
