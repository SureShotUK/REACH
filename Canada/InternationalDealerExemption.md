## Executive Summary

The **International Dealer Exemption** (Section 8.18 of NI 31-103 and Sections 39/46 of NI 93-101) allows foreign dealers to trade with sophisticated Canadian clients without obtaining full Canadian registration, **subject to strict conditions**.

### Key Points

**Purpose:**
- Allows UK <a href="https://www.fca.org.uk/" target="_blank">FCA</a>-regulated firms to offer derivatives to Canadian professional clients
- Avoids full <a href="https://www.osc.ca/" target="_blank">OSC</a> registration and <a href="https://www.ciro.ca/" target="_blank">CIRO</a> membership requirements
- Relies on home jurisdiction (UK <a href="https://www.fca.org.uk/" target="_blank">FCA</a>) regulation

**Fundamental Limitation:**
- **ONLY available to dealers operating from foreign jurisdiction WITHOUT physical presence in Canada**
- Physical offices or permanent staff in Canada **disqualify** the exemption
- Must deal exclusively with Permitted Clients / Eligible Derivatives Parties (EDPs)

**Critical Compliance Issue:**
- Standard International Dealer Exemption **does NOT permit physical offices in Canada**
- Firms with Canadian offices require **full dealer registration** OR **specific exemptive relief order** allowing physical presence

## What is the International Dealer Exemption

### Legal Basis

**<a href="https://www.osc.ca/en/securities-law/instruments-rules-policies/3/31-103/unofficial-consolidation-national-instrument-31-103-registration-requirements-exemptions-and-3" target="_blank">National Instrument 31-103 - Registration Requirements, Exemptions and Ongoing Registrant Obligations</a>:**
- Section 8.18: International dealer

**<a href="https://www.osc.ca/en/securities-law/instruments-rules-policies/9/93-101/multilateral-instrument-93-101-derivatives-business-conduct" target="_blank">National Instrument 93-101 - Derivatives: Business Conduct</a>:**
- Section 39: Exemption for unregistered international dealers
- Section 46: Exemption for certain unregistered international derivatives dealers

### Purpose and Philosophy

The exemption recognizes that:

1. **Sophisticated investors** (permitted clients/EDPs) can protect themselves without full regulatory oversight
2. **Home jurisdiction regulation** (UK FCA) provides adequate supervision of dealer
3. **Market access** should be facilitated for global derivatives markets
4. **Canadian jurisdiction** can still be maintained through agent for service and submission requirements

### Who Can Use It

**Eligible dealers:**
- Registered in a "specified foreign jurisdiction" (includes UK)
- Authorized by home regulator (FCA) to conduct same activities
- Dealing exclusively with permitted clients or EDPs
- **Operating from home jurisdiction without physical presence in Canada** (standard exemption)

**UK FCA-regulated firms qualify** as specified foreign jurisdiction.

### What It Allows

**Permitted activities:**
- Trading derivatives with Canadian permitted clients/EDPs
- Soliciting Canadian clients (if they meet permitted client criteria)
- Providing derivatives to sophisticated institutional and high-net-worth investors

**NOT permitted:**
- Retail client solicitation or trading
- Dealing with clients below permitted client thresholds
- Operating from physical Canadian office (under standard exemption)

## Physical Presence - Critical Compliance Issue

### The Standard Rule

**International Dealer Exemption (<a href="https://www.osc.ca/en/securities-law/instruments-rules-policies/3/31-103/unofficial-consolidation-national-instrument-31-103-registration-requirements-exemptions-and-3" target="_blank">Section 8.18 NI 31-103</a>) is ONLY available to dealers:**

> "...who do not have a place of business in the local jurisdiction and are not otherwise required to be registered as a dealer in the local jurisdiction."

**Key Term:** "Place of business in the local jurisdiction"

### What Constitutes Physical Presence

**Factors indicating physical presence that DISQUALIFY standard exemption:**

✗ **Leased or owned office space** in Canada
✗ **Employees or agents working from Canadian location**
✗ **Business address or phone number** in Canada
✗ **Holding out as having Canadian office**
✗ **Regular in-person client meetings** at Canadian location

### What Does NOT Constitute Physical Presence

**Activities that generally do NOT disqualify exemption:**

✓ **Occasional business travel** to Canada for client meetings
✓ **Attending conferences** or industry events in Canada
✓ **Having clients located in Canada** (with all interactions remote from UK)
✓ **Using third-party service providers** in Canada (if not acting as dealer's agents)
✓ **Agent for service** (required lawyer/firm to receive legal notices)

### Why Physical Presence Matters

**If dealer has physical presence in Canada:**

**Standard International Dealer Exemption:** ✗ NOT AVAILABLE

**Required instead:**
- **FULL dealer registration** with provincial securities commission (<a href="https://www.osc.ca/" target="_blank">OSC</a>, <a href="https://www.bcsc.bc.ca/" target="_blank">BCSC</a>)
- **<a href="https://www.ciro.ca/" target="_blank">CIRO</a> membership** (Canadian Investment Regulatory Organization)
- **Capital requirements, insurance, compliance systems**
- **Registered individuals** (Chief Compliance Officer, Ultimate Designated Person)
- **6-12 month registration process**
- **Significant ongoing costs and regulatory burden**

**OR:**

- **Specific exemptive relief order** from securities commission
- Custom terms and conditions allowing physical presence
- Typically granted case-by-case with specific justification
- Must apply for and receive formal order/decision

### Consequences of Physical Presence with Only Standard Exemption

**If operating with physical presence while relying on standard exemption:**

**Legal status:** Operating as **unregistered dealer** (illegal)

**Penalties:**
- **Criminal:** Up to C$5 million fine + 5 years imprisonment
- **Administrative:** Up to C$1 million per violation
- **Civil:** Client contracts potentially voidable, clients can demand refunds

**Business impact:**
- Cannot enforce derivatives contracts against Canadian counterparties
- OSC can freeze operations immediately
- Reputational damage, regulatory reporting to FCA

## Permitted Clients and Eligible Derivatives Parties

### Legal Requirement

International Dealer Exemption requires dealing **EXCLUSIVELY** with clients meeting either:

- **"Permitted Client"** definition (NI 31-103, Section 1.1), OR
- **"Eligible Derivatives Party" (EDP)** definition (NI 93-101, Section 1)

Trading with any client NOT meeting these criteria **violates the exemption** and constitutes illegal unregistered dealing.

### Permitted Client Categories (<a href="https://www.osc.ca/en/securities-law/instruments-rules-policies/3/31-103/unofficial-consolidation-national-instrument-31-103-registration-requirements-exemptions-and-3" target="_blank">NI 31-103 s.1.1</a>)

**Institutional Permitted Clients:**

1. **Canadian financial institutions:**
   - Banks, trust companies, loan companies
   - Insurance companies
   - Registered dealers and advisers

2. **Government entities:**
   - Federal, provincial, territorial governments
   - Crown corporations
   - Municipal corporations

3. **Pension funds:**
   - Regulated by federal or provincial pension legislation
   - Minimum C$25 million in net assets

4. **Registered entities:**
   - Dealers registered in any Canadian jurisdiction
   - Advisers registered in any Canadian jurisdiction
   - Investment fund managers

**High-Net-Worth Individual Permitted Clients:**

5. **Individuals with financial assets exceeding C$5 million:**
   - **"Financial assets"** = cash, securities, derivatives, contracts of insurance
   - **Aggregate realizable value** (not book value)
   - Must verify through net worth statement or financial documentation
   - Can be individual alone OR with spouse (combined assets)

**Corporate/Entity Permitted Clients:**

6. **Entities with net assets of at least C$25 million:**
   - Corporations, trusts, partnerships
   - **"Net assets"** = total assets minus total liabilities
   - Must verify through financial statements

7. **Entities wholly-owned by permitted clients:**
   - All owners are themselves permitted clients
   - Includes holding companies, SPVs

**Other Permitted Clients:**

8. **Registered charities** (under Income Tax Act) meeting asset thresholds

9. **Certain investment funds** and managed accounts

### Eligible Derivatives Party (<a href="https://www.osc.ca/en/securities-law/instruments-rules-policies/9/93-101/multilateral-instrument-93-101-derivatives-business-conduct" target="_blank">NI 93-101 s.1</a>)

**Definition is similar to permitted client but specific to derivatives context:**

**Categories include:**

1. **Financial institutions** (same as permitted client)

2. **Government entities** (same as permitted client)

3. **Pension funds** with C$25 million+ net assets

4. **Individuals with C$5 million+ financial assets**

5. **Entities with C$25 million+ net assets**

6. **Derivatives dealers and advisers** (registered or exempt)

7. **Commercial hedgers:**
   - Entities using derivatives to **hedge risks arising from their business operations**
   - Must have physical commodity exposure or related business risk
   - Important for oil market: Many oil producers, consumers, refiners qualify

### Client Qualification Procedures

**Best practices for verifying permitted client status:**

**For Individuals (C$5 million test):**
- Obtain signed **net worth statement**
- Request supporting documentation (account statements, valuations)
- Update verification annually or when material changes occur
- Document reliance on client representations

**For Entities (C$25 million test):**
- Obtain **audited or reviewed financial statements**
- Verify net assets calculation (assets minus liabilities)
- Update annually
- Document verification process

**For Commercial Hedgers:**
- Understand client's business operations
- Verify derivatives use is for **hedging purposes** (not speculation)
- Document hedging relationship to underlying business risks
- Review annually

**For Registered Entities:**
- Verify registration status on provincial regulator websites
- Check registration is current and in good standing
- Document verification

## Exemption vs. Full Registration Comparison

### International Dealer Exemption

**Advantages:**
- ✓ No full registration process (6-12 months saved)
- ✓ No CIRO membership required
- ✓ Lower compliance costs
- ✓ Rely on FCA home regulation
- ✓ Faster market entry

**Limitations:**
- ✗ **Permitted clients/EDPs only** (no retail, strict thresholds)
- ✗ **No physical presence in Canada** (standard exemption)
- ✗ Fewer regulatory privileges than registered dealer
- ✗ Clients informed you are "unregistered"
- ✗ Still subject to OSC jurisdiction and enforcement
- ✗ Annual filings and fees required

**Costs (approximate):**
- Legal counsel for exemption setup: C$15,000 - C$30,000
- Agent for service: C$2,000 - C$5,000 annually
- OSC participation fees: Based on revenue
- Ongoing compliance: Moderate

### Full Dealer Registration

**Advantages:**
- ✓ Can operate from Canadian offices
- ✓ Can hire Canadian staff
- ✓ Broader client base (potentially retail if registered for it)
- ✓ Full dealer status and privileges
- ✓ No client restrictions beyond suitability
- ✓ More established regulatory framework

**Limitations:**
- ✗ **6-12 month registration process** (application, review, approval)
- ✗ **CIRO membership required** (registration, fees, oversight)
- ✗ **Capital requirements** (minimum C$50,000+, higher for derivatives)
- ✗ **Insurance requirements** (bonding, E&O insurance)
- ✗ **Registered individuals required** (CCO, UDP, dealing representatives)
- ✗ **Proficiency requirements** (CSC, CPH, derivatives courses)
- ✗ **Extensive compliance systems** (policies, procedures, books and records)
- ✗ **Ongoing regulatory reporting** (financial statements, client reporting, trade reporting)
- ✗ **CIRO audits and examinations**

**Costs (approximate):**
- Registration application: C$50,000 - C$150,000 (legal, consulting)
- Capital requirements: C$50,000+ (tied up in business)
- CIRO membership and fees: C$10,000 - C$25,000 annually
- Insurance and bonding: C$15,000 - C$50,000 annually
- Compliance systems: C$50,000+ (initial setup)
- Ongoing compliance staff: C$100,000+ annually (CCO, staff)
- **Total first year:** C$300,000 - C$500,000+
- **Ongoing annual:** C$150,000 - C$300,000+

### Decision Matrix

**When International Dealer Exemption Makes Sense:**

✓ Operating from UK with remote client service
✓ Client base entirely institutional/high-net-worth
✓ Limited Canadian revenue (fees proportional to business)
✓ FCA regulation provides adequate oversight
✓ No need for Canadian retail market access
✓ **No physical presence in Canada**

**When Full Registration Required:**

✓ **Physical offices in Canada** (mandatory requirement)
✓ Canadian staff conducting dealer activities
✓ Significant Canadian business (revenue justifies costs)
✓ Need retail market access
✓ Long-term commitment to Canadian market
✓ Cannot meet permitted client restrictions
