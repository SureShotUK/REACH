## 1. Background: Operation Epic Fury

### Overview
**Operation Epic Fury** is the US Department of Defense codename for a joint US-Israeli military campaign against the Islamic Republic of Iran, launched in the early hours of **Saturday, 28 February 2026**. Israel designated its component **"Operation Roaring Lion."** It is the largest joint US-Israeli military operation in history, targeting at least nine Iranian cities simultaneously including Tehran, Isfahan, Qom, Karaj, and Kermanshah.

Notably, just 27 hours before strikes began, Oman's Foreign Minister announced Iran had agreed to zero stockpiling of enriched uranium with full IAEA verification — a diplomatic breakthrough that was bypassed by the military operation.

**Sources:** <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2026_Israeli%E2%80%93United_States_strikes_on_Iran" target="_blank">Wikipedia — 2026 Israeli-US Strikes on Iran</a> | <a href="https://www.whitehouse.gov/articles/2026/03/peace-through-strength-president-trump-launches-operation-epic-fury-to-crush-iranian-regime-end-nuclear-threat/" target="_blank">White House Statement</a> | <a href="https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/2/28/peace-within-reach-as-iran-agrees-no-nuclear-material-stockpile-oman-fm" target="_blank">Al Jazeera — Oman diplomacy</a>

### Stated US Objectives (White House)
1. Prevent Iran from acquiring a nuclear weapon
2. Destroy Iran's ballistic missile arsenal and production sites
3. Degrade its proxy networks
4. Annihilate Iran's navy

### Key Targets Struck
- Nuclear enrichment facilities (underground centrifuge halls, weaponisation research)
- Ballistic missile launch complexes and hardened underground bunkers
- IRGC command and control nodes
- Iranian naval vessels (one Jamaran-class corvette confirmed sunk)
- Air defence systems and communications infrastructure
- Senior regime leadership — **Supreme Leader Khamenei killed**; 40+ senior IRGC commanders confirmed killed, including Chief of Staff General Abdolrahim Mousavi
- Over **1,000 targets** struck in the first 24 hours

**Current status (2 March 2026):** The Strait is not formally closed but is experiencing a **de facto commercial shipping shutdown**. Iran's IRGC has warned against transit; Maersk, MSC, and Hapag-Lloyd have suspended all Hormuz operations. Analysts warn of potential triple-digit oil prices if disruption persists. The 20 million bpd that transits Hormuz represents approximately 19.3% of total global consumption.

## 3. Summary Assessment

Operation Epic Fury marks a fundamental strategic inflection point in Middle East geopolitics. The killing of Supreme Leader Khamenei and the destruction of Iran's nuclear infrastructure and ballistic missile arsenal removes the Islamic Republic's principal deterrent capabilities. However, the operation was launched despite active diplomatic progress (the Oman-brokered talks) and has triggered immediate consequences for global energy markets.

**The critical near-term risk** is the Strait of Hormuz. Even a partial sustained disruption to the **20 million bpd** (20% of global consumption) that transits the Strait — compounded by the removal of Iran's own **~3.1–3.3 million bpd** of crude from the market — creates significant upward pressure on oil prices. The pre-conflict global supply surplus of ~1.9 mb/d provides limited cushion. Saudi Arabia retains spare capacity of approximately 1.4 million bpd but lacks alternative export routes sufficient to compensate for Hormuz closure at scale.

The **Middle East as a whole produces ~30% of global oil** and hosts ~**10 million bpd of refining capacity**. Iran's **~2.4 million bpd** of refining capacity is itself at risk of direct disruption from the strikes. The situation remains highly fluid as of 2 March 2026.
