## PART 1 — PRECURSOR: THE TWELVE-DAY WAR (JUNE 2025)

---

### 13 June 2025 — Israel launches strikes on Iran; Twelve-Day War begins
In the early hours, the IDF attacked Iranian nuclear facilities, military bases, and infrastructure. Over 200 Israeli fighter jets launched five waves of airstrikes, dropping more than 330 munitions on approximately 100 targets including the Natanz nuclear enrichment facility. Mossad operatives simultaneously sabotaged Iran's air defence systems.

- **Source:** <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Twelve-Day_War" target="_blank">Wikipedia — Twelve-Day War</a>
- **Source:** <a href="https://www.britannica.com/event/12-Day-War" target="_blank">Britannica — 12-Day War</a>

---

### 13–22 June 2025 — Iranian retaliation and US involvement
Iran launched 550+ ballistic missiles and 1,000+ drones at Israel. Four top Iranian generals killed including IRGC Chief Mohammad Bagheri and Commander Hossein Salami. As fighting escalated, the United States intervened, targeting the most fortified sites in Iran's nuclear programme.

- **Source:** <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Twelve-Day_War" target="_blank">Wikipedia — Twelve-Day War</a>

---

### 22 June 2025 — US strikes Natanz, Fordow, and Isfahan
US forces struck Iran's three main nuclear enrichment facilities. Trump claimed the attacks "degraded" Iran's nuclear programme; Iranian officials acknowledged setbacks but denied the programme was destroyed.

- **Source:** <a href="https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/2/28/us-israel-bomb-iran-a-timeline-of-talks-and-threats-leading-up-to-attacks" target="_blank">Al Jazeera — Timeline of talks and threats</a>

---

### 23 June 2025 — Iran fires missiles at Al Udeid Air Base (Qatar)
Missiles targeted the Al Udeid Air Base in Qatar housing US soldiers. All intercepted; no casualties reported.

- **Source:** <a href="https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/2/28/us-israel-bomb-iran-a-timeline-of-talks-and-threats-leading-up-to-attacks" target="_blank">Al Jazeera — Timeline of talks and threats</a>

### 24 June 2025 — US-brokered ceasefire ends Twelve-Day War
A ceasefire mediated by Oman took effect, ending 12 days of fighting. Iran reported at least 610 citizens killed; Israel claimed 28 fatalities. Total casualties: approximately 1,060–1,190 Iranian deaths, 33 Israeli deaths.

## PART 4 — THE WAR BEGINS: DAY 1 (28 FEBRUARY 2026)

---

### 28 February 2026, ~06:35 UTC — CENTCOM announces airstrikes; Operation Epic Fury begins
At approximately 06:35 UTC, US Central Command announced airstrikes against Iran. US warships launched Tomahawk cruise missiles and US Army HIMARS launchers were deployed. The Israeli Air Force launched what it called its largest combat sortie in history. Approximately 200 Israeli fighter jets and US B-2 Spirit stealth bombers, B-1 Lancers, and B-52 Stratofortresses struck targets simultaneously across Iran. Israel designated its component Operation Roaring Lion; the US codenamed it Operation Epic Fury.

**Targets struck in the first wave:**
- Compound of Leadership House (Pasteur Street district, Tehran) — home of Supreme Leader Khamenei, the presidential palace, and the National Security Council
- Iran's Air Force Headquarters, Operations Command, and Intelligence Command
- Air defence systems and missile launchers across western and central Iran
- 500 military targets across Iran in the first 12 hours; over 1,200 bombs dropped in 24 hours

- **Source:** <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2026_Israeli%E2%80%93United_States_strikes_on_Iran" target="_blank">Wikipedia — 2026 Israeli-US strikes on Iran</a>

### 28 February 2026 — Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei killed
Supreme Leader Ayatollah Ali Khamenei, 86, was killed in the opening strike on the Leadership House compound in Tehran. Also killed: Defence Minister Aziz Nasirzadeh, IRGC Commander Mohammad Pakpour, Defence Council Secretary Ali Shamkhani, and four top Ministry of Intelligence officials. Khamenei's daughter, son-in-law, grandchild, and daughter-in-law Zahra Haddad-Adel were also killed. Iranian state media confirmed his death early on 1 March; 40 days of national mourning declared.

- **Source:** <a href="https://www.npr.org/2026/02/28/nx-s1-5730158/israel-iran-strikes-trump-us" target="_blank">NPR — Khamenei killed</a>

### 28 February 2026 — Minab girls' school destroyed; 175–180 killed (mostly children)
A missile struck the Shajareh Tayyebeh girls' elementary school in Minab, Hormozgan province in southern Iran. Between 175 and 180 people were killed, the vast majority of them schoolchildren. This became the deadliest single incident of the conflict to date. The school was located adjacent to a naval base.

Iran launched hundreds of ballistic missiles and drones at Israel and at US military bases across the region. Targets included:
- **Israel:** Air raid sirens activated across Israel; 48-hour nationwide state of emergency declared; Israeli airspace closed; hospitals moved to underground compounds

## PART 7 — DAYS 6–10 (5–9 MARCH 2026)

---

### 6–7 March 2026 — Over 400 Iranian targets struck; Mehrabad Airport hit
Israeli Air Force operations on 6–7 March struck over 400 targets with approximately 1,465 munitions. More than 80 fighter jets attacked Tehran alone, dropping ~230 munitions. Key strikes included:
- 16 cargo aircraft destroyed at Mehrabad Airport (used by IRGC Quds Force)
- Fuel depots in Shahr Rey, Shahran, Nobonyad
- IRGC university and missile production site
- Shahid Dastgheib Airport in Shiraz (at least 5 Iranian military aircraft destroyed)
- Ballistic missile production sites in Parchin and Shahroud
- Strikes in Karaj, Sanandaj, Yazd, Qazvin, Tabriz

- **Source:** <a href="https://israel-alma.org/daily-report-the-second-iran-war-march-8-2026-1800/" target="_blank">Alma Research — March 8 daily report</a>

### 24 March 2026 — Pakistan delivers US 15-point ceasefire plan to Iran
Pakistan's army chief Field Marshal Syed Asim Munir delivered President Trump's 15-point ceasefire proposal to Iran via Pakistani intermediaries. The plan broadly covers:
- Dismantlement of Iran's nuclear programme; no enrichment on Iranian soil
- Transfer of enriched material to the IAEA; decommission of Natanz, Isfahan, Fordow
- Abandonment of proxy armed networks (Hezbollah, Houthis)
- Limits on missile programmes
- Strait of Hormuz remains open to international shipping
- In exchange: lifting of nuclear-related US sanctions; assistance with civilian nuclear programme at Bushehr; removal of snapback mechanism
- Proposal of a one-month interim ceasefire while details are negotiated

Pakistan offered to host in-person US-Iran talks in Islamabad. Pakistan PM Shehbaz Sharif stated Pakistan was ready "to facilitate meaningful and conclusive talks." Egypt and Turkey were also relaying messages between parties.

### 25 March 2026 — Iran formally rejects US 15-point ceasefire plan; issues 5-condition counterproposal
Iran formally rejected Trump's 15-point ceasefire plan through state Press TV, citing an anonymous senior security official: "Iran will end the war when it decides to do so and when its own conditions are met." An Iranian military spokesperson stated: "Someone like us will never come to terms with someone like you. Not now, not ever."

**Iran's 5-condition counterproposal:**
1. Full lifting of all US sanctions
2. War reparations paid to Iran
3. Iranian sovereignty over the Strait of Hormuz (including right to collect fees)
4. Unrestricted continuation of Iran's missile programme
5. Legal guarantees that aggression against Iran will not be repeated (plus cessation of Israeli strikes on Hezbollah)
