## Summary

The chart illustrates the Plan-Do-Check-Act (PDCA) cycle for Quality Management System (QMS) implementation, as outlined in ISO 9001. It shows a structured approach to quality management, emphasizing continuous improvement through planning, execution, evaluation, and refinement of processes. The flow is circular, indicating an iterative process that integrates customer requirements, organizational context, and stakeholder expectations into the QMS framework.

## Flow Description as Numbered Steps

1. **Start with "Organization and its context"** — The process begins by considering the organization's external and internal environment, including customer requirements and stakeholder expectations.

2. **Move to "Planning (6)"** — Based on the organizational context, planning is initiated to define quality objectives, strategies, and processes required for achieving them.

3. **Proceed to "Leadership (5)"** — Leadership ensures that a quality policy and objectives are established, aligned with the organization's strategic direction, and communicated throughout the organization.

4. **Transition to "Support (7), Operation (8)"** — Support activities include providing resources, competence, awareness, communication, and control of documents and operational processes. Operational processes are executed according to planned arrangements, ensuring that products and services meet specified requirements.

5. **Move to "Performance evaluation (9)"** — Performance is monitored, measured, analyzed, and evaluated against quality objectives and customer satisfaction levels. This step includes internal audits, management reviews, and addressing nonconformities.

6. **Proceed to "Improvement (10)"** — Based on the performance evaluation, actions are taken to improve processes, products, services, and overall QMS effectiveness. This may involve corrective action or preventive measures.

7. **Return to "Organization and its context"** — The cycle continues iteratively, with ongoing consideration of organizational changes, customer requirements, and stakeholder expectations, ensuring continuous improvement.
