# Schematic Representation of the Elements of a Single Process

**From ISO9001.pdf this is a description of the image found in the document.**

## Summary

This chart describes the fundamental structure of any single process in a management system (likely following ISO-style process methodology). It illustrates how processes receive inputs from predecessor processes, transform those inputs through activities, produce outputs, and deliver them to subsequent processes or receivers. The model emphasizes that all these elements are subject to controls and checkpoints for monitoring and measuring performance.

## Numbered Flow Steps

1. **Sources of Inputs** — The process begins at the "Sources of Inputs" block, which represents PREDECESSOR PROCESSES. These sources include:
   - Providers (internal or external)
   - Customers
   - Other relevant interested parties

2. **Sources of Inputs → Inputs** — Inputs flow from the sources into the process. The inputs consist of:
   - MATTER
   - ENERGY
   - INFORMATION
   - For example, in the form of materials, resources, requirements

3. **Inputs → Activities** — The inputs enter the Activities phase at the Starting point. The activities transform or process these inputs.

4. **Activities (internal flow)** — Within the Activities shape, work proceeds from the Starting point (left side) to the End point (right side).

5. **Activities → Outputs** — Once activities are complete (at the End point), outputs are generated. The outputs consist of:
   - MATTER
   - ENERGY
   - INFORMATION
   - For example, in the form of product, service, decision

6. **Outputs → Receivers of Outputs** — The outputs flow to receivers, which represent SUBSEQUENT PROCESSES. These receivers include:
   - Customers (internal or external)
   - Other relevant interested parties

## Parallel Paths and Cross-Cutting Elements

**Controls and Checkpoints** (bottom centre box):

- A single box labeled **Possible controls and check points to monitor and measure performance** sits below the entire flow.
- Five arrows point upward from this box to each of the main elements:
  - Sources of Inputs
  - Inputs
  - Activities
  - Outputs
  - Receivers of Outputs
- This indicates that monitoring and measurement controls can be applied at every stage of the process, not just at one point.

## Key Facts

- Process **inputs** include three categories: Matter, Energy, and Information (e.g. materials, resources, requirements).
- Process **outputs** also include three categories: Matter, Energy, and Information (e.g. product, service, decision).
- **Sources of inputs** are predecessor processes located at providers (internal or external), customers, or other relevant interested parties.
- **Receivers of outputs** are subsequent processes located at customers (internal or external) or other relevant interested parties.
- The **Activities** phase has a defined Starting point and End point.
- **Controls and checkpoints** can be placed at any point in the process — sources, inputs, activities, outputs, or receivers — to monitor and measure performance.
- This is a **universal process model**, applicable to any single process regardless of industry or function.
