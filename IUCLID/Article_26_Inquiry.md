## Executive Summary

### What is an Article 26 Inquiry?

An **Article 26 Inquiry** is a mandatory pre-registration notification to the HSE that:
- Notifies HSE of your intent to register HVO under UK REACH
- Grants access to the Substance Information Exchange Forum (SIEF)
- Reveals existing UK REACH registrants for the substance
- **Enables you to identify and contact registrants for Letter of Access (LoA) negotiations**

### Why Submit an Inquiry for HVO?

**Purpose:** To identify existing HVO registrants so you can:
1. Contact lead registrants or consortium managers
2. Negotiate Letter of Access to reference existing registration data
3. Join the HVO consortium rather than conducting independent registration
4. Avoid duplicating expensive vertebrate animal studies (legally required)

### HVO Substance Details

**For your Article 26 Inquiry, you'll need these identifiers:**

- **Substance Name:** Renewable hydrocarbons (diesel type fraction)
- **Common Name:** HVO - Hydrotreated Vegetable Oil
- **EC Number:** 700-571-2
- **CAS Number:** 928771-01-1
- **Molecular Formula:** C10-20H22-42
- **Substance Type:** UVCB (Unknown or Variable composition, Complex reaction products or Biological materials)
- **Known UK REACH Registration:** UK-01-9638319484-0-0004 (existing registration)

### Timeline Considerations

**CRITICAL: As a New Importer Without Transitional Status:**

- **No "Deadline"** - You simply cannot import until registered
- **You MUST complete registration BEFORE first import of ≥1 tonne**
- **No transitional period applies** (October 2028 deadline is irrelevant to you)
- **Registration is required before you can legally start your business importing HVO**

**Time Required for Complete Process:**

**Phase 1: Article 26 Inquiry**
- IUCLID preparation: 1-2 weeks (first time, with guidance)
- HSE processing: 2-4 weeks
- **Total: 3-6 weeks**

**Phase 2: Letter of Access Negotiation**
- Identify registrants: 1-2 weeks after inquiry approval
- Negotiate LoA terms: 1-3 months
- **Total: 1.5-3.5 months**

**Phase 3: Full Registration**
- Prepare member dossier: 1-2 months
- Submit and HSE review: 1-3 months
- **Total: 2-5 months**

**Total Timeline: 6-9 months from starting inquiry to registration approval**

**Recommended Urgent Timeline:**
- **Now (February 2026):** Start Article 26 Inquiry immediately
- **March 2026:** Inquiry approved, registrants identified
- **April-June 2026:** Negotiate and secure Letter of Access
- **July-August 2026:** Prepare full registration dossier
- **September 2026:** Submit full registration
- **November-December 2026:** Registration approved
- **January 2027:** ✓ Can legally start importing HVO

**Do not delay - you cannot generate revenue from HVO imports until registration is complete.**

## PART 4: Completing Essential IUCLID Sections

**For an Article 26 Inquiry, you must complete minimum sections to establish substance identity and confirm analytical characterization.**

### 4.1 Section 1.1: Identification

**Purpose:** Establish unique chemical identity of HVO

#### Step 1: Link Reference Substance

**HVO may or may not have an IUCLID reference substance. Try this first:**

1. In Section 1.1, click **"Add reference substance"**
2. Search IUCLID inventory for:
   - EC Number: "700-571-2"
   - CAS Number: "928771-01-1"
   - Name: "Renewable hydrocarbons"

**If Reference Substance Exists:**
- Select it from search results
- This auto-populates EC/CAS numbers, IUPAC name, molecular formula
- ✓ Proceed to Section 1.2

**If Reference Substance Does NOT Exist:**
- You must manually enter substance identifiers
- Continue with manual entry below

#### Step 2: Manual Entry of Identifiers (if no reference substance)

**Required Fields:**

**EC Number:**
- Enter: `700-571-2`

**CAS Number:**
- Enter: `928771-01-1`

**IUPAC Name:**
- Enter: `Renewable hydrocarbons (diesel type fraction)`

**Molecular Formula:**
- Enter: `C10-20H22-42`
- Note: This indicates alkanes with 10-20 carbons

**Molecular Weight Range:**
- Enter: `142-282 g/mol`
- (Corresponds to C10 to C20 alkanes)

**SMILES (if available):**
- UVCB substances may not have single SMILES notation
- Leave blank or enter representative structure if known

### 4.2 Section 1.2: Composition

**⚠️ CRITICAL SECTION - Must be complete for dossier creation**

#### Step 1: Add Composition Entry

1. Navigate to Section 1.2 in your substance dataset
2. Click **"Add block"** or **"Add composition"**
3. Select type: **"Legal entity composition of the substance"**

#### Step 2: Define Composition Type

**For HVO:**
- Composition type: **"UVCB"**
- Rationale: HVO is a complex mixture of hydrocarbons derived from biological feedstocks
- Composition varies depending on feedstock and processing

#### Step 3: Add Constituents

**HVO Typical Constituents:**

HVO consists primarily of paraffinic hydrocarbons. Add the main constituent(s):

**Constituent 1: n-Alkanes (C15-C18)**
- Click **"Add constituent"**
- Name: n-Alkanes C15-C18
- Typical concentration: 60-80%
- Concentration range: 60-80% w/w

**Constituent 2: Iso-alkanes (C15-C18)**
- Click **"Add constituent"**
- Name: Iso-alkanes C15-C18
- Typical concentration: 15-30%
- Concentration range: 15-30% w/w

**Constituent 3: Other hydrocarbons**
- Name: Cycloalkanes and other hydrocarbons
- Typical concentration: 5-15%
- Concentration range: 5-15% w/w

**⚠️ Important:** Your actual HVO composition will depend on:
- Feedstock source (used cooking oil, palm oil, rapeseed oil, etc.)
- Hydrotreatment process conditions
- Refer to your supplier's Certificate of Analysis (CoA) for exact composition

#### Step 4: Degree of Purity

**For HVO:**
- Purity: Typically >99% renewable hydrocarbons
- Main impurities: May contain trace amounts of sulfur, nitrogen compounds
- Specify based on your analytical data

#### Step 5: Structural Formula (Optional but Recommended)

**Upload structural formula image:**

1. Click **"Add attachment"** in structural formula field
2. Upload image file showing representative HVO structure
3. **Accepted formats:** JPG, JPEG, GIF, PNG
4. **Recommended:** Simple 2D structure showing typical n-alkane (e.g., C16H34)

**How to Create Structure Image:**
- Use ChemDraw, MarvinSketch, or similar software
- Draw representative structure (e.g., n-hexadecane)
- Add note: "Representative structure - HVO is UVCB mixture"
- Export as JPG or PNG (300 DPI recommended)

### 4.3 Section 1.4: Analytical Information

**Purpose:** Provide analytical evidence confirming your HVO substance identity and composition

#### Required Analytical Data

**1. Appearance:**
- Physical state: Liquid
- Color: Colorless to pale yellow
- Odor: Mild hydrocarbon odor

**2. Analytical Methods Used:**

Check boxes for methods you have data for:
- ✓ **GC-MS** (Gas Chromatography-Mass Spectrometry) - Primary method for HVO
- ✓ **GC-FID** (Gas Chromatography-Flame Ionization Detector) - Hydrocarbon profiling
- ✓ **HPLC** (High-Performance Liquid Chromatography) - If used
- ✓ **FTIR** (Fourier Transform Infrared Spectroscopy) - Functional group identification
- ✓ **NMR** (Nuclear Magnetic Resonance) - If available
- ✓ **Elemental Analysis** - C, H, N, S content

**3. Attach Supporting Documents:**

**Certificate of Analysis (CoA):**
1. Obtain CoA from your HVO supplier
2. **Sanitize the document:**
   - Remove supplier name if confidential
   - Remove pricing information
   - Remove proprietary manufacturing details
   - Keep: analytical results, test methods, specifications
3. Save as PDF
4. Click **"Add attachment"** in Section 1.4
5. Upload sanitized CoA
6. Title: "HVO Certificate of Analysis"
7. Description: "Analytical data confirming substance identity and composition"

**Spectroscopic Data (if available):**
- FTIR spectrum (PDF or image)
- NMR spectra (if conducted)
- GC-MS chromatogram (showing hydrocarbon distribution)

**Specification Sheet:**
- Technical data sheet from supplier
- Should include: density, viscosity, flash point, cetane number
- Helps confirm substance identity

#### Section 1.4 Text Fields

**Analytical Data Interpretation:**
- Briefly describe how analytical data confirms substance identity
- Example: "GC-MS analysis shows hydrocarbon distribution consistent with C10-C20 paraffinic diesel fraction. FTIR confirms absence of aromatic compounds and presence of aliphatic C-H bonds. Composition matches expected profile for hydrotreated vegetable oil."

**Batch-to-Batch Consistency:**
- Describe expected variability in HVO composition
- Example: "Composition varies slightly depending on feedstock blend (UCO, palm, rapeseed). All batches meet EN 15940 paraffinic diesel specification."

### 4.4 Section 1.3: Identifiers (Optional but Recommended)

**Additional Identifiers to Include:**

**Other Names:**
- HVO
- Hydrotreated Vegetable Oil
- NExBTL Renewable Diesel (if Neste product)
- Renewable Diesel
- Paraffinic diesel

**Trade Names (if applicable):**
- List any commercial product names your HVO is sold under
- Example: "Neste MY Renewable Diesel", "Greenergy HVO", etc.

### 4.5 Section 1.5: Classification and Labelling (Optional for Inquiry)

**UK CLP Classification for HVO:**

**Hazard Classes:**
- **Flam. Liq. 3 - H226:** Flammable liquid and vapour
- **Asp. Tox. 1 - H304:** May be fatal if swallowed and enters airways

**Additional Statement:**
- **EUH066:** Repeated exposure may cause skin dryness or cracking

**Signal Word:** Danger

**Pictograms:**
- GHS02 (Flame)
- GHS08 (Health hazard)

**Note:** Classification is not mandatory for Article 26 Inquiry but is good practice and will be required for full registration.

## PART 5: Validate the Substance Dataset

**⚠️ Before attempting to create a dossier, ALWAYS validate first.**

### 5.1 Run Validation Assistant

**Navigation:**
1. Open your HVO substance dataset
2. Click **"Validate"** button (toolbar or right panel)
3. Select validation type: **"Submission Checks"**
   - This checks for critical errors that block submission
4. Optionally also run: **"Quality Checks"**
   - This identifies warnings and data inconsistencies

### 5.2 Review Validation Results

**Understanding the Report:**

**Failures (Red) ❌**
- MUST be fixed before dossier creation
- Will prevent submission to HSE
- Examples:
  - Missing reference substance or identifiers
  - Incomplete composition
  - Missing analytical data
  - Legal Entity not assigned

**Warnings (Amber) ⚠️**
- Should be addressed but won't block submission
- May indicate incomplete or inconsistent data
- Review and fix if possible

**Passed (Green) ✓**
- Requirements met
- No action needed

### 5.3 Fix All Failures

**Process:**

1. Click on each failure in the validation report
   - Report provides hyperlinks directly to problem sections
2. Navigate to the flagged section
3. Add missing data or correct errors
4. Save the substance dataset
5. Re-run validation
6. Repeat until zero failures remain

**Common Failures for HVO:**

**"Reference substance not linked or identifiers missing"**
- Fix: Complete Section 1.1 with all HVO identifiers (EC 700-571-2, CAS 928771-01-1)

**"Composition not provided"**
- Fix: Complete Section 1.2 with UVCB composition including constituent ranges

**"Analytical data missing"**
- Fix: Add analytical methods in Section 1.4 and attach CoA

**"Legal Entity not assigned"**
- Fix: Edit substance dataset properties, set Owner to your Legal Entity

**"Working Context not set"**
- Fix: Set Working Context to "REACH" or "UK REACH" (upper-right corner)

### 5.4 Target: Zero Failures

**Goal:** Achieve zero failures in Submission Checks before proceeding to dossier creation.

**Save your validated dataset** - you're ready for Part 6.

   - Keep backup copies

   - Typical inquiry: 5-20 MB

## PART 9: Troubleshooting Common Issues

### Issue 1: "Create Dossier" Button Greyed Out or Missing

**Symptom:** Cannot create dossier from substance dataset

**Possible Causes and Solutions:**

**1. No Working Context Set**
- **Check:** Upper-right corner of substance dataset - is "REACH" or "UK REACH" selected?
- **Fix:** Click Working Context dropdown, select "REACH" or "UK REACH"
- **Result:** "Create Dossier" should become available

**2. No Legal Entity Assigned as Owner**
- **Check:** Substance dataset properties - is Owner field blank?
- **Fix:**
  - Edit substance dataset
  - Set Owner to your Legal Entity
  - Save dataset
- **Result:** Owner should show your company name

**3. Legal Entity Missing or Incomplete**
- **Check:** Main menu → Legal Entities - do you have at least one entity?
- **Fix:**
  - Create or import Legal Entity (see Part 2)
  - Ensure Legal Entity sections 1.1, 1.2 are complete
- **Result:** Legal Entity appears in substance Owner dropdown

**4. Substance Dataset Not Saved**
- **Check:** Is there an unsaved changes indicator?
- **Fix:** Press Ctrl+S or click Save button
- **Result:** Changes saved, try dossier creation again

**5. Critical Validation Failures**
- **Check:** Run Validation Assistant on dataset
- **Fix:** Resolve all red failures in Sections 1.1, 1.2, 1.4
- **Result:** Zero failures, dossier creation enabled

**6. IUCLID Trial Account Limitations**
- **Check:** Account type - is this a trial account?
- **Fix:** Ensure account has "Full Access" role
- **Workaround:** Trial accounts CAN create dossiers - if issue persists, contact IUCLID support

**7. Browser or Cache Issues**
- **Check:** Try different browser or incognito mode
- **Fix:**
  - Clear browser cache
  - Refresh page (F5 or Ctrl+R)
  - Try Chrome, Firefox, or Edge
- **Result:** Interface updates, button appears

### Issue 2: Validation Failures

**Common failures specific to HVO:**

**"Reference substance not linked"**
- **Cause:** Section 1.1 missing HVO identifiers
- **Fix:**
  - Try searching IUCLID inventory for EC 700-571-2
  - If not found, manually enter: EC 700-571-2, CAS 928771-01-1, IUPAC name, molecular formula
- **Verify:** Section 1.1 shows all identifiers

**"Composition not provided"**
- **Cause:** Section 1.2 incomplete or missing constituents
- **Fix:**
  - Add "Legal entity composition of the substance"
  - Select substance type: UVCB
  - Add at least one constituent (e.g., n-Alkanes C15-C18, 60-80%)
  - Specify concentration ranges
- **Verify:** Section 1.2 shows composition with percentages

**"Analytical data missing"**
- **Cause:** Section 1.4 empty or incomplete
- **Fix:**
  - Add appearance description
  - Check analytical methods used (GC-MS, FTIR, etc.)
  - Attach sanitized CoA or analytical reports
- **Verify:** Section 1.4 shows methods and attachments

**"Degree of purity not specified"**
- **Cause:** Missing purity information in Section 1.2
- **Fix:** Add purity percentage (e.g., >99%)
- **Verify:** Purity field populated

**"Working Context not set"**
- **Cause:** No regulatory context selected
- **Fix:** Upper-right corner, select "REACH" or "UK REACH"
- **Verify:** Working Context displays selected value

### Issue 3: Cannot Export Dossier
